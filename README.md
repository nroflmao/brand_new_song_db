# The Brand New Lyrical Universe: Master Connection Guide

The discography of Brand New, penned primarily by frontman Jesse Lacey, operates as a massive, 16-year interconnected narrative. Rather than isolated songs, the albums function as a continuous conversation where metaphors evolve, promises are broken, and early arrogant curses return as tragic realities.

This guide tracks the five major motifs that define the band's universe: **The Car Crash, The Water/Sinking, Anatomy/Deconstruction, The Silence of God,** and **Isolation (The Bed).**

Here is the chronological evolution of the Brand New lyrical universe.

---

## SECTION 1: *Your Favorite Weapon* (2001) – The Weaponized Origins

On their debut album, Jesse Lacey uses dark imagery as a weapon against his enemies. He wishes car crashes, drowning, and isolation upon ex-friends and ex-lovers. By later albums, these exact curses will happen to him, forming the basis of his crushing guilt complex.

*   **The Car Crash:** The entire motif begins as a petty insult. In **"Seventy Times 7,"** Jesse wishes a fatal car crash on an ex-best friend: *"I hope there's ice on all the roads / And you can think of me when you forget your seatbelt / And again when your head goes through the windshield."* (This directly sets up the real-life trauma of *Limousine*).
*   **The Water/Sinking:** In **"Jude Law and a Semester Abroad,"** he hopes his ex-girlfriend meets her end by *"drowning at the bottom of the sea."* In **"The Shower Scene,"** he warns, *"These are the things I saved to sink you."* 
*   **The Anatomy/Surgical:** The edgy teenage lyric from **"The Shower Scene"** (*"My wrist is sore from pulling at your insides all night"*) acts as the prototype for the clinical deconstruction of the self seen later in *Degausser* and *Same Logic/Teeth*.
*   **The "Salad Days" Illusion:** **"Soco Amaretto Lime"** acts as the anchor point for the band's lost youth. Jesse vows to *"stay 18 forever"* and mocks adults who spend their nights *"growing old in your bed."* (This song is explicitly referenced and rejected 16 years later in *Waste*).

---

## SECTION 2: *Deja Entendu* (2003) – The Arrogant Realization

*Deja Entendu* is the transitional era. The metaphors move from being weapons used on others to warnings about himself. He begins to realize his own toxicity, manipulating people in cars and hotels, and prophesying his own tragic end.

*   **The Genesis of Sinking:** The album literally opens with the line, *"I'm sinkin' like a stone in the sea"* (**"Tautou"**). This introduces the "stone" imagery that dominates *Millstone*. 
*   **The Shipwreck:** **"Play Crack the Sky"** establishes the band's ultimate nautical tragedy. The ship goes down because *"the wrong words will strand you / Come off course while you sleep."* The outro introduces the lifeline: *"You know that you are not alone."*
*   **The "Die Alone" Prophecy:** In **"I Will Play My Game...,"** Jesse states, *"Everyone who lives will someday die and die alone."* He begins to fear his own isolation.
*   **The Bed as a Trap:** He mocks adults in *Soco*, but in *Deja*, the bed becomes a place of predatory behavior (**"Me vs. Maradona vs. Elvis"**) or desperate self-harm (**"The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot"**: *"Sneak to your bed to pour salt in your wounds"*). 
*   **The Arrogance of Grace:** In **"Okay I Believe You...,"** he arrogantly plays God, claiming, *"This is the grace only we can bestow."*

---

## SECTION 3: *The Leaked Demos & Singles* (2006) – The Missing Links

The unreleased tracks bridge the gap between *Deja's* arrogance and *TDAG's* absolute breakdown. 

*   **Locked Out of Heaven:** In **"1996,"** Jesse confirms his fear of divine rejection: *"If there's any justice in Heaven / Then God won't let me in / He'll lock the gates."* This explains why he is terrified of the afterlife in later albums.
*   **The "Cut Me Up" Command:** **"Nobody Moves"** features the lyric, *"Please cut me up,"* moving the surgical metaphor from emotional to physical, predicting the mutilation in *Same Logic/Teeth*. 
*   **The Flood and the Getaway:** **"Nobody Moves"** also merges the car and water motifs: *"The road is washed out / From the river that flooded."*

---

## SECTION 4: *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me* (2006) – The Tragic Breakdown

The curses of *Your Favorite Weapon* have come true. The car crash is no longer a joke; it is a literal tragedy. The sinking is no longer a metaphor; it is an execution. God has officially stopped listening.

*   **The Car Crash (Reality):** **"Limousine"** recounts the real-life death of a 7-year-old girl killed by a drunk driver (*"He's volunteered with grace to end your life"*). The road is now a place of meaningless death (**"Millstone"**: *"He just ran out in the street"*), and Jesse eventually wants to drive his own car off a bridge (**"Handcuffs"**).
*   **The Sinking (The Execution):** In **"Millstone,"** he becomes the weapon: *"I'm my own stone around my neck."* In **"Luca,"** the drowning becomes an assassination: *"Fixed you with cement galoshes... swimming alone in the lake."* In **"Jesus Christ,"** the ship from *Play Crack the Sky* finally sinks *"in sight of land."*
*   **The Bureaucracy of God:** Heaven is an office, and it is closed. **"Millstone"** (*"Used to pray like God was listening"*); **"Degausser"** (*"Some saint got the job of writing down my sins"*); **"Limousine"** (Jesus says, *"Never again"*); **"You Won't Know"** (*"Completely out of their forgiveness supplies"*).
*   **The Anatomy Command:** In **"Degausser,"** Jesse obsessively instructs himself to break down: *"Take apart your bed / Take apart your head / Take apart your friends."*

---

## SECTION 5: *Daisy* (2009) – The Rock-Bottom Surrender

If *TDAG* was the panic of falling apart, *Daisy* is the exhausted apathy of living in the wreckage. Jesse stops fighting the water and lets himself drown.

*   **Cars Become Trains:** Cars can be steered, but trains are inevitable. The vehicle motif shifts to total powerlessness. **"Gasoline"** (*"Jumping towards a train"*); **"Bought a Bride"** (*"Laid her on the train tracks"*); **"Noro"** (*"Our car's stuck on the train crossing"*).
*   **Apathetic Drowning:** He returns to the lake from *Luca*, but now his execution is complete. **"At the Bottom"** (*"At the bottom you'll find all my friends / They don't swim 'cause they're all dead"*). In **"Sink,"** he gives up: *"You want to sink, so I'm gonna let you."* 
*   **The Grave:** The stone from *Millstone* shatters. **"In a Jar"** (*"Stones in your eyes / Stones in your mouth"*). He spends the album burying things, turning to *"dirt and dust"* (**"At the Bottom"**).
*   **The Final Condemnation:** Having been locked out of heaven (*1996*), he accepts his fate. **"Noro"** closes the album with: *"I'm on my way to hell."*

---

## SECTION 6: *Science Fiction* (2017) – The Therapy & Resolution

Brand New’s final album acts as a capstone project. Framing the record as a 400-hour intensive therapy session, Jesse Lacey systematically addresses and resolves the traumas from 2001 to 2009. 

*   **The "Salad Days" Resolution:** In **"Waste,"** Jesse looks back at the teenage promise of *Soco Amaretto Lime* ("stay 18 forever") and dismisses it: *"Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."*
*   **The Crash Resolution:** He accepts the damage. In **"Waste,"** he is *"Laying in the road / Shoe-in for the crash of the day."* In **"Can't Get It Out,"** he admits, *"You had me caught in your headlights / You were running me down,"* but he survives it.
*   **The Sinking Resolution:** After 16 years of drowning, he makes peace with the ocean. In **"Could Never Be Heaven,"** he is at the bottom of the lake, but it is calm. In **"Same Logic/Teeth,"** he notes that *"Fish won't judge you by your faults."* In **"Waste,"** he offers the ultimate cure: *"Turn to water and we'd all float on."*
*   **The Anatomy Resolution:** He finally fulfills the command from *Degausser*. **"Same Logic/Teeth"** (*"You decide to make incisions... glued them back together / Found out you did it wrong"*). In **"Waste,"** taking himself apart is finally an act of healing: *"Take your head apart, free your own heart."*
*   **The Isolation Resolution:** The terrifying prophecy of *Deja* ("everyone dies alone") and *TDAG* ("I will die all alone") is defeated. In **"Could Never Be Heaven,"** he realizes his family is a part of him (*"We are not separate"*). In **"Waste,"** he reclaims the outro of *Play Crack the Sky*, repeating the mantra: *"You are not alone, you get to reset."*
*   **The Grace Resolution:** He accepts the Silence of God. In **"Waste,"** he acknowledges he spent years *"begging for some grace,"* but accepts that *"we get no reply."* He no longer needs heaven's forgiveness supplies; he has forgiven himself.

# Track 1: "The Shower Scene" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
As the opening track of Brand New's entire discography, "The Shower Scene" establishes the baseline for Jesse Lacey's lyrical universe. In this era, the recurring motifs (water, body horror, destruction) are entirely **weaponized**. He isn't suffering from these things yet; he is actively inflicting them on his enemies. This song lays the groundwork for the karmic guilt that will crush him on later albums.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Water / Sinking Motif (The Weapon)

*   **The Lyric:** *"These are the things I saved to sink you"*
*   **The Connection:** Here, sinking is an aggressive, intentional attack used to destroy an ex-friend/lover. 
*   **The Evolution:** By the time he reaches adulthood, this weapon is turned entirely inward. He goes from sinking *others* to realizing he is sinking *himself* (**Millstone**: *"I'm my own stone around my neck / This ship of fools I'm on will sink"*). By *Daisy*, the sinking is a fatalistic execution (**At the Bottom**: *"At the bottom you'll find all my friends"*). Finally, in *Science Fiction*, he learns to stop sinking and survive (**Waste**: *"You could turn to water and we'd all float on"*).

#### 2. The Anatomy & Surgery Motif (Body Horror)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Go tell him how my wrist is sore from pulling at your insides all night"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the absolute origin point of Jesse's obsession with dissecting the human body. In 2001, it is used as a visceral, edgy insult to describe emotional damage (or physical intimacy) that leaves him "sore."
*   **The Evolution:** This desire to "pull at insides" evolves into a clinical, desperate need to deconstruct himself to find out what is wrong with his brain. It traces directly to **Deja Entendu** (**I Will Play My Game...**: *"Watch me as I cut myself wide open on this stage"*), peaks in the manic panic of **TDAG** (**Degausser**: *"Take apart your head"*), and ends in the literal self-surgery of **Science Fiction** (**Same Logic/Teeth**: *"So you decide to make incisions at your home... glued them back together, you found out you did it wrong"*).

#### 3. The Direct *Science Fiction* Callback ("Hide Your Daughters")

*   **The Lyric (Background/Bridge):** *"Hide your daughters"* (Note: While often left out of standard lyric sheets, this phrase is famously shouted in the background vocals during the bridge of the studio recording of this song).
*   **The Connection:** 16 years later, Brand New explicitly quotes this exact moment on their final album. 
*   **The Evolution:** In **Science Fiction** (**In The Water**), Jesse sings: *"Hide your daughters, the old men say / You were young once before, you know how we get our way."* It is a direct, meta-apology for the toxic, manipulative teenage behavior he exhibited in songs exactly like "The Shower Scene." He also warns fathers about himself in **You Won't Know** (*"Your daughters weren't careful / I fear that I am a slippery slope"*).

#### 4. The Explosion Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"So draw or throw and I will explode / I hope she's caught in the explosion"*
*   **The Connection:** Wishing an explosive death on someone who wronged him. 
*   **The Evolution:** This explosive imagery becomes literal, tragic trauma in **Limousine** (*"You explode / You firefly"*), describing the fatal car crash. Later, he turns the explosion into global nuclear annihilation in **137** (*"Let's all go play Nagasaki / We can all get vaporized"*).

# Track 2: "Jude Law and a Semester Abroad" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Jude Law" is a defining anthem of teenage bitterness, but beneath the pop-punk sneer lies the exact blueprint for Brand New's darkest imagery. Once again, Jesse is aiming the band's core motifs (crashes, burning, drowning) outward at an ex-girlfriend. The absolute irony of this song is that the horrific fate he wishes upon her—"drowning at the bottom of the sea"—becomes the exact metaphor he uses to describe his own severe depression just a few years later.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Sinking Motif (The Genesis of "The Bottom")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Or drowning at the bottom of the sea"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the most direct, prophetic lyrical seed on the entire album. In 2001, drowning at the bottom of the sea is his ultimate, spiteful fantasy for an ex. 
*   **The Evolution:** By *Deja Entendu*, he applies this to himself (**Tautou**: *"I'm sinkin' like a stone in the sea"*). By *TDAG*, the "stone" pulls him all the way down (**Millstone**). By *Daisy*, he is literally writing a song called **At the Bottom** (*"And there's a lake / And at the bottom you'll find all my friends"*). What started as a curse on an enemy in "Jude Law" became his own permanent psychological address until *Science Fiction*.

#### 2. The Vehicle Crash & Wreckage Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"And even if her plane crashes tonight / She'll find some way to disappoint me / By not burning in the wreckage"*
*   **The Connection:** Expanding the car crash motif to aviation, Jesse continues to weaponize fatal accidents.
*   **The Evolution:** The specific word "wreckage" returns with massive guilt on *TDAG*. In **You Won't Know**, he sings to a loved one: *"You can't say you were right / When you're just another pile of wreckage in the light."* The burning wreckage he wishes for here becomes the literal nightmare he is trapped in during **Limousine** and **You Stole** (*"Now the flames are burning me in my bed"*).

#### 3. Substance Abuse & Biblical Imagery (The Flood)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Whatever poison's in this bottle... I got a plan to drink for 40 days and 40 nights"*
*   **The Connection:** He frames his drinking not just as a binge, but as a biblical event. "40 days and 40 nights" refers to the great flood in Genesis. 
*   **The Evolution:** The combination of alcohol and tragedy is central to the band's mythology. The poison in the bottle here sets up the "all-American" drunk driver in **Limousine**, the daily "tripping out" and "coming down" in **Waste**, and the literal flood he builds in **At the Bottom** (*"Now I'm drowning in the flood I made"*).

#### 4. The "Final Song" Lie (The Meta-Narrative)

*   **The Lyric:** *"No more songs about you / After this one, I am done"*
*   **The Connection:** Jesse routinely breaks the fourth wall to talk about the act of songwriting itself, often lying to the audience (and himself) about his intentions.
*   **The Evolution:** He was not, in fact, "done." He repeats this exact same meta-promise on *Deja Entendu* (**Okay I Believe You...**: *"Every line is about who I don't wanna write about anymore"*). By *Science Fiction*, he looks back at all of these early songs and claims and sighs in **Waste**: *"Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."*

# Track 3: "Sudden Death in Carolina" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Sudden Death in Carolina" is steeped in the language of true crime, murder, and dramatic self-destruction. While the song is fundamentally about a toxic, on-again/off-again teenage relationship, Jesse frames it as a literal homicide. It introduces vital visual elements to the Brand New database: the isolation of the bed, the "backseat" of the car, and the visceral imagery of fire and hanging.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Fire / Burning Motif (Internalized)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Last night I swallowed liquor and a lighter / And this morning I threw up fire but it's nothing new"*
*   **The Connection:** Here, fire is used as a dramatic, almost cartoonish metaphor for a brutal hangover and teenage heartbreak. 
*   **The Evolution:** The "fire" quickly becomes a symbol for his soul, purity, and eventual punishment. In **Millstone**, he mourns that he *"used to be such a burning example."* In **You Won't Know**, he admits he *"burned the bush that covered my light."* Finally, on *Science Fiction*, this specific lyric comes full circle in **Lit Me Up**: *"Lit me up and I burned from the inside out / Yeah, I burned like a witch in a Puritan town."* The fire he threw up in 2001 consumes him entirely by 2017.

#### 2. The Car / Backseat Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"And every slow and quiet car ride I spent drinking in the backseat"*
*   **The Connection:** The car is presented as a space of teenage melancholy and secretive drinking. 
*   **The Evolution:** The "backseat" specifically becomes a recurring site of powerlessness and moral failing. In **Handcuffs** (*TDAG*), the backseat is a trap: *"I'd throw you down in the back seat / As if you'd committed a terrible crime."* Even more directly, it links to **Archers** (*TDAG*), where the car ride represents their doomed trajectory: *"So we strap ourselves up to this engine now / With a god who we found laying under the backseat."*

#### 3. The Bed / Isolation Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"And this quiet dark bed feels like the middle of nowhere"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the very first time the "Bed" motif appears as a place of profound isolation. Even though he is lying next to a girl (he mentions "the back of your head"), he feels completely alone.
*   **The Evolution:** This feeling metastasizes into the crushing loneliness of his adulthood. It predicts **Jesus Christ** (*"But with nobody in your bed / The night's hard to get through"*), **Millstone** (*"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*), and **At the Bottom** (*"I'm alone now in my bed"*). The bed in the Brand New universe is almost never a place of rest; it is a place of torment.

#### 4. The Execution Motif (Hanging & The River)

*   **The Lyric:** *"They hung her from the bridge on Monday... They dropped her body in the river"*
*   **The Connection:** As an act of mental revenge, he imagines the town forming a mob and executing the girl who broke his heart via hanging and drowning.
*   **The Evolution:** Just like the car crashes and the sinking, the execution eventually turns inward. The hanging returns in **You Won't Know** when he calls for his own death (*"Hey hey hey, Mr. Hangman / Go get your rope"*). The mob dropping the body in the river perfectly predicts the mob execution he suffers in **Luca** (*"So we fixed you with cement galoshes / And no one can save you now"*).

#### 5. The Gun Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"Cause her lips taste like a loaded gun"*
*   **The Connection:** A classic noir metaphor for a dangerous romance.
*   **The Evolution:** Guns and weapons transition from romantic metaphors to symbols of paranoia and apocalypse. In **Gasoline** (*Daisy*), it represents cowardice (*"Before the kids could tell their dog goodbye / Well, you were loading up your gun"*). In **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*), the gun is the tool of a paranoid, radicalized father (*"Last night I heard a voice that said, 'Don't give up your gun'"*).

# Track 4: "Mix Tape" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Mix Tape" is the most musically stripped-down track on *Your Favorite Weapon*, and lyrically, it is pure, distilled adolescent resentment. It lacks the violent imagery (crashes, drowning, fire) of the surrounding songs, focusing instead on themes of authenticity, communication, and growing up. However, its core message—the failure to effectively communicate and the bitter weaponization of words—lays the foundation for one of Jesse's most defining personal struggles on later albums.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Communication Failure Motif (Hidden Meanings)

*   **The Lyric:** *"But when I say, 'Let's keep in touch' / I really mean, 'I wish that you'd grow up'"*
*   **The Connection:** Here, Jesse boasts about his ability to say one thing while meaning another. He uses polite communication to mask deep insults.
*   **The Evolution:** This smug ability to lie and manipulate words eventually destroys him. By *Deja Entendu*, he admits his words are toxic (**Okay I Believe You...**: *"My tongue's the only muscle on my body that works harder than my heart"*). By *TDAG*, the inability to say what he means becomes a source of total despair (**Degausser**: *"I'm on my own, I'll never say anything right"*; **Jesus Christ**: *"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique"*). He goes from bragging about his hidden meanings to wishing he could just be understood.

#### 2. The Critique of "Growing Up"

*   **The Lyric:** *"I really mean, 'I wish that you'd grow up'"*
*   **The Connection:** In the context of *YFW*, he accuses the girl of acting fake/immature, demanding she "grow up."
*   **The Evolution:** The concept of "growing up" is fraught throughout Brand New's discography. In **Soco Amaretto Lime**, he completely reverses this stance, vowing to *"stay eighteen forever."* By *Deja*, growing up is framed as a grim, unwanted inevitability (**Sic Transit Gloria**: *"The act becomes the art of growing up"*). He demands she grow up here, but spends the next three albums terrified of doing it himself.

#### 3. The Shadow Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *(Background Vocals)* *"Yeah, but I wish you were my shadow"*
*   **The Connection:** Repeated frantically in the outro, the "shadow" represents a desire for constant presence, possession, or control over the other person.
*   **The Evolution:** The shadow returns as a symbol of inescapable fear and consequence. In the unreleased single **Mene** (released right before *Science Fiction*), he flips the imagery entirely: *"Don't want my fear to become my shadow."* What was once a desire to have someone follow him around (*Mix Tape*) becomes a terror of the darkness that follows him (*Mene*).

#### 4. The Meta-Reference (Music and Authenticity)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And the way you don't appreciate Brand New or me / And I know that you're a sucker for anything acoustic"*
*   **The Connection:** Explicitly name-dropping his own band (in a highly sarcastic, acoustic song) to mock someone else's taste in music.
*   **The Evolution:** This obsession with whether people are authentic, or whether *he* is authentic, plagues his songwriting. In **Okay I Believe You...**, he mocks the audience directly (*"These are the words you wish you wrote down"*). By **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*), the accusation of being a fake/poser is turned entirely inward: *"So how's it feel to walk around like you're some sort of freak? / You're just an actor, but now they caught you."*

***

# Track 5: "Failure By Design" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Failure By Design" introduces a major shift in the Brand New narrative. Up until this point, Jesse’s anger has been directed outward at ex-girlfriends and former friends. In this song, the anger turns violently inward. It is a hyper-caffeinated, anxiety-ridden anthem about writer’s block, procrastination, and the pressure of the music industry. It establishes the "Meta-Songwriting" motif and acts as the prototype for the crushing self-blame that defines his later work.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash Motif (Intentional Destruction)

*   **The Lyric:** *"(I ignore it) what say we go and crash your car?"*
*   **The Connection:** While earlier songs used car crashes as curses upon enemies, here the crash is proposed as an active, deliberate escape from stress and responsibility. 
*   **The Evolution:** This desire to intentionally wreck the car evolves into a terrifying, suicidal urge on *TDAG*. In **Handcuffs**, he expands on this exact thought: *"I'd drive my car off of the bridge / If I knew that you weren't inside... A fantastic way to kill some time."* The flippant question in 2001 becomes a dark fantasy by 2006.

#### 2. Self-Blame & Self-Sabotage (The Origin)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Become a recluse and I blame myself 'cause I / Make things hard and you're just trying to help... I'm sick and sunk, and I blame myself"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the absolute genesis of Jesse Lacey’s guilt complex. The phrase "I blame myself" is the seed that grows into the suffocating weight of *The Devil and God*.
*   **The Evolution:** He realizes that *he* is the problem. This leads directly to **Millstone** (*"I'm my own stone around my neck"*), **At the Bottom** (*"I stole bricks from the dam... drowning in the flood I made"*), and **Archers** (*"You can only blame yourself, is what I say"*). 

#### 3. The "Late Night / No Sleep" Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"This is one more late night basement song... This is one more sleepless night... And we just want sleep, but this night is hell"*
*   **The Connection:** In this track, his insomnia is caused by procrastination and the stress of writing an album. 
*   **The Evolution:** Sleep deprivation becomes a central symbol of his mental health struggles and guilt. By *TDAG*, the inability to sleep is maddening. **Degausser** opens with: *"Goodbye to sleep / I think this staying up is exactly what I need."* By *Daisy*, the insomnia has infected everyone he knows in **Noro**: *"Why doesn't anyone I know sleep? / Are they all just scared of their dreams?"*

#### 4. The Meta-Narrative (Songs About Songwriting)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We don't believe in filler, baby... No one sleeps 'til we get this shit out on the shelves... But this time, I got nothing to say"*
*   **The Connection:** The song breaks the fourth wall to complain about writing the song you are currently listening to. 
*   **The Evolution:** Jesse frequently uses his lyrics to comment on his failure to write meaningful lyrics. This traces directly to **Play Crack the Sky** (*"I spoke the words but never / Gave a thought to what they all could mean"*), **You Stole** (*"I'll pretend like I've got something to say / But I've got nothing"*), and the desperate confession in **In The Water** (*"I can't say it enough / Can't sing it enough... So I'll find another way"*).

#### 5. The "Sinking" Motif (Internalized)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm sick and sunk, and I blame myself"*
*   **The Connection:** Previously, he "saved things to sink you" (*Shower Scene*). Here, for the very first time, *he* is the one who is sunk. 
*   **The Evolution:** This explicitly predicts the manic chorus of **You Won't Know** (*"And when you think I'm sunk I will float on and on"*), the aquatic doom of **Luca**, and the final resolution in **Waste** (*"Turn to water and we'd all float on"*).

# Track 6: "Last Chance To Lose Your Keys" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Last Chance To Lose Your Keys" is a textbook example of teenage defensive mechanisms. Stood up and rejected at a party, Jesse retreats into a shell of feigned apathy. He insists he is "better off" alone, locking himself in his house to prove a point. While the stakes here are incredibly low (high school party drama), the coping mechanisms he displays—isolation, communication blackout, and redefining his identity—become the exact ways he handles profound trauma later in his career.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Isolation / Locked Doors Motif (Defensive Solitude)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Home on a Saturday night / With all my doors locked up tight / I won't be thinking about you, baby"*
*   **The Connection:** Here, isolation is a choice. He locks the doors to protect his bruised ego, pretending he prefers to be alone. 
*   **The Evolution:** This petty, chosen isolation hardens into the inescapable, terrifying loneliness of adulthood. The "locked doors" of his house evolve into the locked gates of Heaven (**1996**: *"God won't let me in / He'll lock the gates"*). The "Saturday night" at home becomes the existential dread of dying alone in his room (**Jesus Christ**: *"But with nobody in your bed / The night's hard to get through... I will die all alone"*). 

#### 2. The Telephone / Communication Breakdown

*   **The Lyric:** *"You called me on the phone / But you don't even wanna talk... Don't pick up the phone"*
*   **The Connection:** The telephone is used here as a tool for petty games. They call but won't speak; he tells her not to bother calling at all. 
*   **The Evolution:** The inability to communicate over a line becomes one of Jesse's most tragic recurring symbols. When he eventually *does* need to make a call for his own survival, the lines are dead. In **You Won't Know**, he panics: *"And I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone."* By **Waste**, he is calling out to God/the universe, but the line is empty: *"Always begging for some grace / When there's no time, we get no reply."*

#### 3. The Rejection of Youth vs. "Salad Days"

*   **The Lyric:** *"Forget everythin' you think you know about me / This isn't high school"*
*   **The Connection:** He aggressively repeats "This isn't high school" to assert his maturity and dismiss the girl's behavior as juvenile.
*   **The Evolution:** Jesse's relationship with youth is incredibly contradictory. Just a few tracks later on this same album (**Soco Amaretto Lime**), he begs to *"stay 18 forever."* In **Sic Transit Gloria** (*Deja*), he tackles *"the art of growing up."* Ultimately, his assertion here that "this isn't high school" is finally put to rest in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he looks back at this entire era of his life and sighs: *"Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."*

#### 4. The Perception / Identity Crisis

*   **The Lyric:** *"Forget everythin' you think you know about me"*
*   **The Connection:** He demands a reset on how he is perceived, frustrated that his attempts to be seen as a mature "good guy" are failing.
*   **The Evolution:** The fear that people think they know him, or that he is secretly a fraud, becomes a massive focal point on later albums. In **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot** (*Deja*), he warns: *"Hope you find out what you are, already know what I am."* By *Science Fiction*, this identity crisis turns into absolute self-loathing in **Same Logic/Teeth**: *"So how's it feel to walk around like you're some sort of freak? / You're just an actor, but now they caught you."*

***

`[Isolation/Locked Doors]` `[Communication/The Telephone]` `[Aging/Growing Up]` `[Perception/Identity]`

# Track 7: "Logan to Government Center" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
Named after a specific subway commute in Boston, "Logan to Government Center" is a track defined by geographical and emotional distance. Jesse is attempting to bridge a gap with a girl who has moved on, using a mix of desperate pleading and massive, unwarranted arrogance ("I'm a national phenomenon"). It heavily reinforces two of the most critical motifs in the Brand New universe: the failure of the telephone, and the inescapable feeling of being "alone."

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Isolation / "Not Alone" Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"You wouldn't get me on the phone / And you couldn't make me not alone"*
*   **The Connection:** Here, he places the burden of his loneliness on someone else. He is alone because *she* failed to fix it. 
*   **The Evolution:** The realization that no one else can fix his loneliness becomes a terrifying awakening. On *Deja Entendu*, he projects this onto everyone (**I Will Play My Game...**: *"Everyone who lives will someday die and die alone"*). On *TDAG*, the fear consumes him completely (**Jesus Christ**: *"I will die all alone / And when I arrive I won't know anyone"*). It takes 16 years for him to finally absolve himself of this fear in **Waste**: *"You are not alone... you get to reset."*

#### 2. The Telephone & Unsent Letters (Communication Failure)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Consider this a letter that I never sent... The phone bills stacked up by my bedside / You wouldn't get me on the phone"*
*   **The Connection:** He is drowning in failed communication. Unsent letters and ignored phone calls define his relationship.
*   **The Evolution:** The telephone remains a symbol of utter futility. In **You Won't Know**, physical death prevents the call (*"And I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone"*). By **Waste**, the phone metaphor upgrades to a spiritual level—he is calling out to God for grace, but the line is dead: *"When there's no time, we get no reply."*

#### 3. The Bedside Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"The phone bills stacked up by my bedside / With your picture, you're looking surprised"*
*   **The Connection:** The bed acts as a physical monument to his failures and obsessions. 
*   **The Evolution:** The bed becomes the primary setting for his trauma. In **Jesus Christ**, the tragedy is having *"nobody in your bed."* In **Millstone**, he acknowledges it's his own fault: *"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it."* In **You Stole** (*Daisy*), the bed is literally catching fire: *"And now the flames are burning me in my bed / But I just don't care."*

#### 4. Arrogance & The Ego

*   **The Lyric:** *"Yeah, you wouldn't get me (you're a blue-eyed lightning bolt) / You wouldn't get me (I'm a national phenomenon)"*
*   **The Connection:** To cover up the pathetic nature of his pleading, he suddenly declares himself a "national phenomenon." 
*   **The Evolution:** This ego trip explodes on *Deja Entendu*. He takes "national phenomenon" a step further and declares himself a god in **Okay I Believe You...** (*"I am heaven sent... This is the grace only we can bestow"*). But this arrogance completely shatters by *TDAG*. In **Sci-Fi**, he looks back at this exact ego with embarrassment in **Can't Get It Out**: *"Got my Messiah impression / I think I got it nailed down."*

#### 5. The Island / Dying Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"My island nights are all spent dying"*
*   **The Connection:** A dramatic description of Long Island (his home), mixing geographical isolation with extreme melodrama.
*   **The Evolution:** The island/water imagery and the concept of "dying" evolve from teen melodrama to visceral reality. The "island nights spent dying" become the literal shipwreck off Long Island in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"Pounded in the shallows off the tip of Montauk Point... the morning finds our bodies washed up thirty miles west"*).

# Track 8: "The No Seatbelt Song" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"The No Seatbelt Song" is the darkest, most submissive track on *Your Favorite Weapon*. While the rest of the album is filled with teenage arrogance and spite, here Jesse strips all of that away, offering himself up to be used, broken, and destroyed by a partner. Crucially, this song introduces the physical imagery of being chained by the neck, and it solidifies the "Car Crash" motif not as a curse upon an enemy, but as a fatalistic, romanticized wreckage that he is actively participating in.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash Motif (Romanticized Wreckage)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We're wrecking and I'm dry like a drum"* (Paired with the title itself: "The No Seatbelt Song").
*   **The Connection:** Choosing not to wear a seatbelt is an active choice to accept fatal consequences. He knows the relationship is crashing, and he is choosing not to protect himself.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the exact precursor to **Seventy Times 7**, where he tells his enemy: *"Think of me when you forget your seatbelt / And again when your head goes through the windshield."* But more importantly, it predicts the tragic apathy of **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he admits he was *"laying in the road / shoe-in for the crash of the day / And we're never going to walk away."* The crash is no longer romantic; it is inevitable destruction.

#### 2. The Chain / The Neck (The Prototype for Millstone)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Fix me to a chain around your neck / And wear me like a nickel"*
*   **The Connection:** He begs to be worn as a cheap token (a nickel) on a chain around someone else's neck. He wants to be their burden.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the direct lyrical ancestor to one of Brand New's most famous lines. In **Millstone** (*TDAG*), the imagery is completely inverted. He is no longer a small token around someone else's neck; he is a massive, crushing weight around his *own* neck: *"I'm my own stone around my neck."* He begged to be a chain, but he became a boulder.

#### 3. The Execution / The Rope

*   **The Lyric:** *"And me fresh out of rope"*
*   **The Connection:** He has reached the end of his tether; he has no more rope to give, or perhaps no rope left to hang himself with.
*   **The Evolution:** The rope imagery returns in full force on *TDAG*. In **You Won't Know**, he finally calls for the executioner: *"Hey hey hey, Mr. Hangman / Go get your rope."* The threat of hanging introduced here is finally carried out.

#### 4. The Anatomy / Breakdown Command

*   **The Lyric:** *"So take me and break me / And make me strong like you... I'm here for you to use, broken and bruised"*
*   **The Connection:** He invites his partner to physically and emotionally break him down.
*   **The Evolution:** He eventually stops asking others to break him and starts doing it himself. This leads directly to the frantic self-destruction commands of **Degausser** (*"Take apart your head"*) and the literal, botched self-surgery of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"So you decide to make incisions... And then you cracked your head / And broke some bones"*).

#### 5. Aviation / The Pilot

*   **The Lyric:** *"I shot the pilot / I'm begging you to fly this for me"*
*   **The Connection:** He has sabotaged his own life/vehicle (shot the pilot) and is begging someone else to take control before they crash. 
*   **The Evolution:** This connects to his constant use of vehicles (cars, ships, planes) to represent his lack of control. In **Play Crack the Sky**, the ship's crew cannot bail out the water. In **Noro**, the car is stuck on the train tracks. In every scenario, he has destroyed the steering mechanism and is bracing for impact.

***

`[Vehicle Crash/No Seatbelt]` `[The Neck/The Chain]` `[Execution/The Rope]` `[Anatomy/Breaking Down]` `[Aviation/Loss of Control]`

# Track 9: "Seventy Times 7" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Seventy Times 7" is the absolute Big Bang of the Brand New lyrical universe. Written about the real-life falling out between Jesse Lacey and Taking Back Sunday's John Nolan, the song is a masterclass in weaponized spite. The title is an ironic reference to Matthew 18:22, where Jesus says to forgive someone "seventy times seven" times. Jesse, famously, refuses to forgive. The horrific curses he hurls in this song—fatal car crashes, suffocating guilt, and the silence of God—will boomerang back to hit him with devastating force on *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash (The Ultimate Curse)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Have another drink and drive yourself home / I hope there's ice on all the roads / And you can think of me when you forget your seatbelt / And again when your head goes through the windshield"*
*   **The Connection:** In 2001, Jesse weaponizes a fatal, drunk-driving car crash as the ultimate insult.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the most critical linkage in the band's history. Five years later, a real drunk driver ("drinking up, he's all-American") kills a 7-year-old girl in **Limousine**. The horror of this real-life crash shatters Jesse's worldview. He realizes the sick irony of what he used to wish on people. By *Science Fiction*, he accepts the karmic punishment in **Waste**: *"Laying in the road / Was a shoe-in for the crash of the day."*

#### 2. The Bureaucracy of Heaven (The Breakdown of Prayer)

*   **The Lyric:** *"They say you need to pray if you want to go to heaven / But they don't tell you what to say when your whole life has gone to hell"*
*   **The Connection:** This introduces the "Silence of God" motif. He is in crisis, but religion offers no practical script or comfort.
*   **The Evolution:** The breakdown of prayer becomes the central theological crisis of the band. In **Millstone**, he admits: *"I used to pray like God was listening."* In **Jesus Christ**, he begs for a sign but fears God's silence. In **Limousine**, Jesus finally speaks just to say *"Never again."* Finally, in **Waste**, he accepts that *"we get no reply."* The confusion of 2001 becomes the exhausted acceptance of 2017.

#### 3. The Bed / Isolation Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"Ignore the sun, the cover's over my head / Wrote a message on my pillow that says 'Jesse, stay asleep in bed'"*
*   **The Connection:** The bed is used as a bunker to hide from reality and depression.
*   **The Evolution:** On *TDAG*, the bed turns from a sanctuary into a site of torment. In **Degausser**, he violently rejects it: *"Goodbye to sleep / I think this staying up is exactly what I need / Well, take apart your bed."* In **Millstone**, he acknowledges the trap: *"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it."* In **You Stole** (*Daisy*), the bed is literally on fire.

#### 4. The Execution / The Rope

*   **The Lyric:** *"Search your shelf for something which to hang yourself"*
*   **The Connection:** Following the car crash, he suggests his former friend commit suicide by hanging.
*   **The Evolution:** Just like the car crash, the hanging curse turns inward. In **You Won't Know** (*TDAG*), he begs for his own execution: *"Hey hey hey, Mr. Hangman / Go get your rope."*

#### 5. Anatomy / Guts & Spines

*   **The Lyric:** *"'Cause I've seen more spine in jellyfish / I've seen more guts in 11-year-old kids"*
*   **The Connection:** He uses biological parts (spines, guts) to insult his enemy's cowardice.
*   **The Evolution:** This biological vocabulary expands massively. In **At the Bottom** (*Daisy*), the guts are literal roadkill: *"Some dogs that got hit by cars / All came to spill their guts."* In **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*), he dissects his own spine and skeleton: *"And then you cracked your head / And broke some bones."* 

#### 6. The Meta-Narrative (Songs About Songs)

*   **The Lyric:** *"As if it happening wasn't enough / I gotta go and write a song just to remind myself how bad it sucked"*
*   **The Connection:** Acknowledging the miserable irony of being a songwriter—he has to immortalize his own pain just to make art.
*   **The Evolution:** This cynical view of his own profession peaks in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"I spoke the words but never / Gave a thought to what they all could mean"*), and **I Will Play My Game...** (*"Watch me as I cut myself wide open on this stage / Yes, I am paid to spill my guts"*).

***

**App/Database Tags for "Seventy Times 7":**
`[The Car Crash/Drunk Driving]` `[Bureaucracy of Heaven/Prayer]` `[The Bed/Sleep]` `[Execution/The Rope]` `[Anatomy/Spines & Guts]` `[Meta: Songwriting]`

# Track 10: "Secondary" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Secondary" is a frantic, self-pitying sprint about feeling like a backup option. It is the sound of teenage inadequacy. While not as violent or metaphor-heavy as "Seventy Times 7," it solidifies Jesse's use of physical injury to describe emotional pain, and it establishes the "Isolation/At Home" motif that will eventually evolve into his profound, existential loneliness.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. Anatomy / Bodily Mutilation

*   **The Lyric:** *"Stop the circulation to my legs and break my bones"*
*   **The Connection:** Rather than asking someone else to break him (like in *The No Seatbelt Song*), he is using extreme physical trauma to describe the paralyzing effect of rejection.
*   **The Evolution:** This desire to be physically broken evolves into the literal self-mutilation detailed in *Science Fiction*. In **Same Logic/Teeth**, he fulfills this exact lyric: *"And then you cracked your head / And broke some bones / And when you glued them back together / You found out you did it wrong."* 

#### 2. The Isolation Motif (Better by Myself)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Dateless and late, I'm better by myself at home"*
*   **The Connection:** Mirroring the sentiment in *Last Chance to Lose Your Keys* ("Home on a Saturday night with all my doors locked up tight"), he uses isolation as a defense mechanism. He pretends he prefers to be alone to mask his rejection.
*   **The Evolution:** This petty, chosen isolation hardens into genuine, terrifying adult loneliness. By *The Devil and God*, being "better by myself" becomes the dread of **Jesus Christ**: *"I will die all alone / And when I arrive I won't know anyone."* It takes until **Waste** for him to finally dismantle this defense mechanism, reassuring himself: *"You are not alone."*

#### 3. Communication Failure (The Wrong Words)

*   **The Lyric:** *"She laughs at every word I know came out completely wrong"*
*   **The Connection:** He attempts to communicate, but fails. His words are mocked or misunderstood.
*   **The Evolution:** The failure of words is a massive recurring theme. In **Play Crack the Sky**, he warns that *"the wrong words will strand you / Come off course while you sleep."* By **Degausser**, he has given up trying to speak correctly at all: *"I'm on my own, I'll never say anything right."*

#### 4. The "Dying Breed" / Existential Fatalism

*   **The Lyric:** *"Past the point of trying, I'm a dying breed... and I'm dying here"*
*   **The Connection:** Melodramatic teenage fatalism. He feels like he is dying because a girl doesn't like him.
*   **The Evolution:** The concept of "dying" transitions from teen melodrama to a very real preoccupation with mortality and hell. In **Daisy** (*At the Bottom*), he says: *"And if I wanted to die before I got old / I should've started some years ago digging that hole."* The hyperbolic "dying here" of 2001 becomes a genuine death wish by 2009.

#### 5. Unread Letters (The Futility of the Medium)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Stop these looks and letters, this isn't for the better... I better hope she got my letters"*
*   **The Connection:** Similar to *Logan to Government Center* ("Consider this a letter that I never sent"), he relies on written correspondence that either isn't sent, isn't received, or makes things worse.
*   **The Evolution:** The failure of the medium (letters, telephones, radios) is constant. In **Play Crack the Sky**, the radio is flooded. In **You Won't Know**, he can't use the telephone. In **Waste**, he begs for grace but gets "no reply."

`[Anatomy/Broken Bones]` `[Isolation/At Home]` `[Communication Failure/Wrong Words]` `[Fatalism/Dying]` `[Unread Letters]`

# Track 11: "Magazines" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Magazines" is arguably the most pop-oriented and straightforwardly adolescent song in the Brand New catalog. It is a fantasy about winning over a girl (named Laetitia), fueled by late-night obsessing and television static. However, even in its most bubblegum moments, it reinforces the core setting of Jesse's misery: the isolated bedroom, the television, and the fear of being left alone forever.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Bedroom / The Bed (The Dead Space)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Broken down in my dead bedroom, stuttering to pictures of you"*
*   **The Connection:** The bedroom is explicitly labeled as "dead." It is a place of breakdown and pathetic obsession.
*   **The Evolution:** This entirely sets the stage for the rest of his career. The "dead bedroom" of 2001 becomes the site of insomnia in **Degausser** (*"Take apart your bed"*), the site of crushing guilt in **Millstone** (*"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*), and the site of literal fire/destruction in **You Stole** (*"And now the flames are burning me in my bed"*).

#### 2. Isolation / The "Alone Forever" Prophecy

*   **The Lyric:** *"Or I'll be left alone forever with my magazines"*
*   **The Connection:** He frames his entire future around a binary choice: either the girl comes to him, or he dies alone.
*   **The Evolution:** This melodramatic teen fear becomes his central adult terror. In **I Will Play My Game...** (*Deja*), he cynically accepts it: *"Everyone who lives will someday die and die alone."* In **Jesus Christ** (*TDAG*), he panics about it: *"I will die all alone / And when I arrive I won't know anyone."* 

#### 3. Television / The Screen as a Substitute

*   **The Lyric:** *"I know that you can always see me, I saw you staring through my TV last night"*
*   **The Connection:** He substitutes real human connection with a parasocial hallucination through a television screen.
*   **The Evolution:** The television and media consumption return as symbols of emotional detachment and fake identity. In **Okay I Believe You...** (*Deja*), he admits his entire persona is fabricated by media: *"And it's all from watching TV / And from speeding up my breathing."* By *Science Fiction*, the TV becomes a tool of paranoia in **451**: *"My TV says 'It's all in your head' / So shake it out and share your bread."*

#### 4. The Open Door vs. The Locked Door

*   **The Lyric:** *"So I'll leave the door open all night / If you decide you want to stop on by"*
*   **The Connection:** In a rare moment of vulnerability on this album, he leaves the door open, waiting for someone to save him from his loneliness.
*   **The Evolution:** This completely contrasts with his defensive posture in **Last Chance to Lose Your Keys** (*"Home on a Saturday night / With all my doors locked up tight"*). Later, in **Failure By Design**, the locked door becomes a symbol of his own self-sabotage: *"And every time I leave, you go and lock the door."*

#### 5. Anatomy / The Shaking Hands

*   **The Lyric:** *"Laetitia, you got my hand shaking"*
*   **The Connection:** A standard pop trope for nervous romance, but it introduces the involuntary physical reaction to stress.
*   **The Evolution:** The physical trembling/shaking returns during the manic breakdown of **Degausser** (*TDAG*): *"I've been on pause, I'm shaking off the rust / I've lost my charge, I've been degaussed."*

# Track 12: "Soco Amaretto Lime" (*Your Favorite Weapon*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
As the acoustic closer to *Your Favorite Weapon*, "Soco Amaretto Lime" is the ultimate "Before" picture. It is a snapshot of pure, unadulterated teenage invincibility. Jesse sings about staying young forever, drinking with friends, and refusing to grow up. Because this song is the literal starting line of his adulthood, almost every song on the subsequent four albums acts as a tragic, exhausted response to the naive promises made on this specific track. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The "Salad Days" Illusion (The Ultimate Callback)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm gonna stay eighteen forever / So we can stay like this forever"*
*   **The Connection:** "Soco" is the sound of the "salad days" (a time of youthful inexperience and innocence). In 2001, Jesse genuinely sang about staying 18 and partying forever. 
*   **The Evolution:** This specific sentiment is directly addressed and destroyed 16 years later on *Science Fiction*. In **Waste**, the older, wiser Jesse looks back at this exact song and sings: *"You and I were stuck in the waste / Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."* He accepts that you cannot stay 18 forever.

#### 2. The Overpass vs. The Road (The Evolution of the Crash)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Passed out on the overpass... Suspended like spirits over speeding cars / You and me were kings over the parkway tonight"*
*   **The Connection:** In "Soco," cars, parkways, and roads are symbols of freedom and teenage rebellion. Passing out on an overpass above traffic is framed as a romantic adventure.
*   **The Evolution:** As Jesse grows up, the road becomes a place of sudden, violent death. The speeding cars they used to watch from the overpass become the machines that destroy them in **Limousine** and **Millstone** (*"He just ran out in the street"*). By **Waste**, the road is a place of total surrender: *"Laying in the road / Was a shoe-in for the crash of the day."*

#### 3. The Bed (The Ultimate Irony)

*   **The Lyric:** *(Background Vocals)* *"You're spending all your nights growing old in your bed"*
*   **The Connection:** In his youth, Jesse mocks adults who stay home and sleep. He wants to "stay awake through summer" and views the bed as a place where boring people go to die.
*   **The Evolution:** The insult he hurls here becomes his own inescapable reality. By *The Devil and God*, he is trapped in that exact bed. He isn't out partying; he is alone, full of guilt, and suffering from insomnia (**Millstone**: *"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*; **At The Bottom**: *"I'm alone now in my bed"*). 

#### 4. The Engine and the End

*   **The Lyric:** *"The low fuel light's been on for days / It doesn't mean anything / I got another 500 miles / Before we shut this engine down"*
*   **The Connection:** In 2001, Jesse arrogantly believed that even when his "fuel" (youth/energy) was running low, he could push the engine indefinitely before shutting it down.
*   **The Evolution:** The engine eventually breaks. The 500 miles run out. By *Science Fiction*, he finally shuts the engine down in **Waste**: *"It's all in your head, your race is run / Don't give up, my son, this is the last one."*

#### 5. "Cut Me Open" (The Surgical Motif Begins)

*   **The Lyric:** *(Background Vocals)* *"Cut me open"*
*   **The Connection:** Sung faintly in the background during the chorus, this is an edgy, dramatic teenage expression of emotion.
*   **The Evolution:** Even in their most innocent song, Jesse slips in lines about bodily dissection. The plea to be "cut open" evolves directly into the obsessive need to deconstruct his mind and body on later albums (**Degausser**: *"Take apart your head"*; **Same Logic/Teeth**: *"So you decide to make incisions"*).

#### 6. The "All-American" Drink

*   **The Lyric:** *"And when I pass the bottle back to Pete... I bet we laugh"*
*   **The Connection:** Drinking (specifically Soco Amaretto Lime, a cheap, sugary mixed drink) is a social, joyful act of bonding.
*   **The Evolution:** Drinking loses its innocence entirely. By **Limousine**, alcohol is the "all-American" poison that causes the fatal crash (*"He's drinking up, he's all-American / And he'll drive"*). By **Waste**, the party is over, and substance abuse is just a sad, isolating cycle: *"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down."*

***

`[Aging/Salad Days]` `[The Road/Overpass]` `[The Bed/Growing Old]` `[The Engine/Fuel]` `[Anatomy/Cut Me Open]` `[Substance Abuse/Drinking]`

# Track 1: "Tautou" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Tautou" serves as the atmospheric prologue to *Deja Entendu*. Composed of just four repeating lines, it is a massive departure from the pop-punk sneer of *Your Favorite Weapon*. Crucially, it introduces the **Water/Sinking** motif not as a weapon used against someone else, but as a personal condition. It is the very first time Jesse Lacey admits that he is the one drowning.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Sinking Stone (The Genesis of Millstone)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm sinkin' like a stone in the sea"*
*   **The Connection:** In *Your Favorite Weapon*, Jesse used sinking as a curse (e.g., *"These are the things I saved to sink you"* in *The Shower Scene*, or *"Drowning at the bottom of the sea"* in *Jude Law*). Here, he applies the curse to himself. 
*   **The Evolution:** This single line invents the defining metaphor of the band's next album. The "stone in the sea" evolves directly into **Millstone** (*TDAG*): *"This ship of fools I'm on will sink / I'm my own stone around my neck."* Later, in *Daisy*, the stone fractures into smaller pieces of crushing weight in **In a Jar**: *"Stones in your eyes / Stones in your mouth."* The sinking finally stops in *Science Fiction*, when he learns to float in **Waste** (*"Turn to water and we'd all float on"*).

#### 2. The Burning Bridge (Internalizing the Fire)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm burning like a bridge for your body"*
*   **The Connection:** Combining the idiom "burning a bridge" (destroying a relationship/path of return) with literal physical destruction. He is consuming himself to reach someone.
*   **The Evolution:** The "fire/burning" motif represents his soul, purity, and eventual self-destruction. In **Sudden Death in Carolina** (*YFW*), he threw up fire. By **You Won't Know** (*TDAG*), he admits he *"burned the bush that covered my light / Even though I'm scared I won't burn that bright."* The fire eventually consumes him completely in **Lit Me Up** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Lit me up and I burned from the inside out."*

#### 3. The Sea / Water Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"In the sea"*
*   **The Connection:** The ocean is introduced here as a vast, consuming force.
*   **The Evolution:** The sea becomes the primary setting for Brand New's greatest tragedies. It is the site of the fatal shipwreck in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"Pounded in the shallows... Of God's good ocean gone wrong"*). In the unreleased track **Mene**, he notes the ocean's terrifying apathy: *"The ocean never sleeps or dreams / It never stops to ponder what it sees."* By *Daisy*, he has become the dead water himself: *"I'm an ocean nothing floats on."*

# Track 2: "Sic Transit Gloria... Glory Fades" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
The title translates from Latin to "Thus passes the glory of the world," which perfectly encapsulates the song's theme: the sudden, traumatic loss of innocence. It tells the story of a boy losing his virginity to a more experienced, predatory partner. Unlike the petty high school drama of *Your Favorite Weapon*, this introduces real physical discomfort, violation, and the terrifying realization that adulthood isn't what he thought it would be. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Bed / Isolation Motif (The Site of Trauma)

*   **The Lyric:** *"A victim, still lying in bed, completely motionless"*
*   **The Connection:** In *Your Favorite Weapon*, the bed was a place to hide from the world (*Seventy Times 7*: "stay asleep in bed") or sulk about a crush (*Magazines*: "dead bedroom"). Here, the bed becomes a place of physical violation and paralysis. 
*   **The Evolution:** This establishes the bed as the primary site of Jesse's adult trauma. In **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot**, he turns into the predator: *"When I sneak to your bed to pour salt in your wounds."* By *TDAG*, the trauma of the bed causes severe insomnia (**Degausser**: *"Take apart your bed"*). By *Daisy*, the bed is a place of total destruction (**You Stole**: *"And now the flames are burning me in my bed"*).

#### 2. The Critique of "Growing Up"

*   **The Lyric:** *"Up the stairs, the station where / The act becomes the art of growing up"*
*   **The Connection:** He frames the loss of virginity (and innocence in general) not as a victory, but as a forced, uncomfortable performance ("the act"). 
*   **The Evolution:** This directly opposes the naivete of **Soco Amaretto Lime** (*YFW*), where he wanted to "stay 18 forever." Here, he is forced to grow up, and he hates it. This weary, cynical view of aging stretches all the way to **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he looks back at the whole process and sighs: *"Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."*

#### 3. Suffocation / The Precursor to Drowning

*   **The Lyric:** *"He's gasping for air... The tickle, the taste of / It used to be the reason I breathed / But now it's choking me up"*
*   **The Connection:** The physical sensation of anxiety and panic is described as suffocation. Something that used to give him life is now killing him.
*   **The Evolution:** This choking imagery perfectly predicts the "Water/Drowning" motif that dominates the rest of their discography. The literal suffocation here turns into the nautical drowning of **Play Crack the Sky** (*"I need you like water in my lungs"*), the heavy weight of **Millstone** (*"I'm my own stone around my neck / Be my breath"*), and the concrete execution in **Luca** (*"There'll still be no air to breathe"*).

#### 4. The Death Wish / Fatalism

*   **The Lyric:** *"Die young and save yourself"*
*   **The Connection:** A darkly cynical piece of advice. He suggests that dying before you have to experience the pain of adulthood ("growing up") is the only way to save your soul.
*   **The Evolution:** This throwaway pessimistic chorus line becomes a literal death wish on *Daisy*. In **At the Bottom**, he echoes this exact sentiment: *"And if I wanted to die before I got old / I should've started some years ago digging that hole."* 

#### 5. The "Meaningful Relationship" Illusion

*   **The Lyric:** *"Unprepared for a life full of lies and failing relationships... So much more than he could ever give / A life free of lies and a meaningful relationship"*
*   **The Connection:** The boy in the song is looking for something real, but is thrust into a world of physical use and deception.
*   **The Evolution:** This fear of lying and failing at relationships becomes his reality. On the very next album, he ruins his relationships because of his own lies (**Jesus Christ**: *"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique"*). It takes until *Science Fiction* for him to finally find that meaningful relationship and save his soul (**Could Never Be Heaven**: *"My wife's heart my own heart / I've heard the outer darkness is really nice this time of year"*).

# Track 3: "I Will Play My Game Beneath the Spin Light" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
This track marks the absolute inception of the "Meta-Songwriting" and "Touring Exhaustion" motifs that will plague Jesse Lacey's career. It is a cynical, exhausted look at the music industry, portraying performing not as an art form, but as a grueling transaction where he is paid to bleed for the entertainment of others. It also introduces the crushing, prophetic realization that he will eventually die alone. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Isolation / "Die Alone" Prophecy

*   **The Lyric:** *"Collect calls to home to tell them that I realize / That everyone who lives will someday die and die alone"*
*   **The Connection:** This is a massive pivot from *Your Favorite Weapon*. In *YFW*, isolation was petty ("Home on a Saturday night... I won't be thinking about you"). Here, isolation becomes a terrifying, inescapable existential truth. 
*   **The Evolution:** This specific line is the seed for the entirety of **Jesus Christ** (*TDAG*). The "realization" he has here becomes his greatest fear three years later: *"And I will die all alone / And when I arrive I won't know anyone."* It takes until 2017 for him to finally defeat this fear in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"You are not alone, you get to reset."*

#### 2. The Anatomy / Surgical Motif (Spilling Guts)

*   **The Lyric:** *"My secrets for a buck / Watch me as I cut myself wide open on this stage / Yes, I am paid to spill my guts"*
*   **The Connection:** He frames emotional vulnerability as literal, physical disembowelment. The audience pays to watch him bleed.
*   **The Evolution:** This introduces the visceral body horror that defines later albums. "Spilling my guts" becomes literal roadkill in **At the Bottom** (*"Some dogs that got hit by cars / All came to spill their guts"*). The act of cutting himself open evolves into the obsessive self-dissection of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"So you decide to make incisions... glued them back together, you found out you did it wrong"*).

#### 3. The Sea / Water Motif (The Harpoon & The Cage)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I won't see home till spring, oh, I would kill for the Atlantic... The harpoon is loaded. The cage is lowered, the water is red"*
*   **The Connection:** He uses intense, predatory nautical imagery (shark diving/whaling) to describe the hostile environment of touring and relationships. 
*   **The Evolution:** This directly sets up the brutal aquatic execution in **Luca** (*"raw bleeding bait... swimming alone in the lake"*). The "red water" also connects to the drowning and bloodletting motifs that stretch through *Daisy* and *Science Fiction*.

#### 4. The Meta-Narrative (Selling Sadness)

*   **The Lyric:** *"But I am paid to make girls panic while I sing"*
*   **The Connection:** A highly cynical breakdown of his own job. He realizes his pain is a commodity. 
*   **The Evolution:** This guilt over exploiting his own (and others') sadness haunts him. In **Okay I Believe You...**, he mocks the fans who buy into it (*"These are the words you wish you wrote down"*). By *Science Fiction*, the guilt of his success crushes him in **In The Water**: *"Drowning in the praise / Never had a chance to break apart."*

#### 5. The Empty Church (Religious Burnout)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Every minute is a mile. I've never felt so hollow / I'm an old abandoned church with broken pews and empty aisles"*
*   **The Connection:** The first use of architectural religious imagery to describe his own spiritual emptiness.
*   **The Evolution:** This directly predicts the "Silence of God" motif. He feels like an empty church here; by **Millstone**, he realizes God has left the building (*"I used to pray like God was listening"*). By **Limousine**, Jesus is *"bored and tired"* of his laments.

#### 6. The "We Won't Let You In" Chorus

*   **The Lyric:** *"And we won't let you in though we're down and out / No we won't let you in, you win, you win, you win"*
*   **The Connection:** A defensive wall against the outside world, the industry, or a specific person. He surrenders ("you win") but refuses to open up.
*   **The Evolution:** This defensive posture is a recurring reaction to stress. It echoes the locked doors of **Failure By Design** and **Last Chance to Lose Your Keys**. Ultimately, he realizes he *has* to let people in to survive, which he finally does in **Science Fiction** (**Could Never Be Heaven**: *"You are not alone / We are not separate"*).

# Track 4: "Okay I Believe You, But My Tommy Gun Don't" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
Named after a line from the fictional gangster movie in *Home Alone*, this track is the absolute peak of Jesse Lacey’s lyrical arrogance. It is a cynical, hyper-articulate dismantling of the music scene, his fans, and his own ego. He plays the role of a manipulative mastermind, bragging about his ability to lie and control people with his words. However, the god-like complex he constructs here will completely shatter on the next album, turning this song into a tragic display of hubris.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Arrogance of Grace (The False Messiah)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I am heaven sent / Don't you dare forget... This is the grace only we can bestow"*
*   **The Connection:** Jesse places himself in the role of God. He claims *he* is the one who grants grace and salvation to his audience.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the ultimate hubris before the fall. On the very next album (*TDAG*), a real tragedy strikes (**Limousine**), and he realizes he has no power. He begs the *actual* Messiah for help, but Jesus says, *"Never again."* By *Science Fiction*, he looks back at the arrogance of "Tommy Gun" with deep embarrassment in **Can't Get It Out**: *"Got my Messiah impression / I think I got it nailed down."* By **Waste**, he is the one *"begging for some grace"* and getting *"no reply."*

#### 2. The Weaponized Tongue (Communication)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Coordinate brain to mouth... Oh, my tongue's the only muscle on my body that works harder than my heart"*
*   **The Connection:** He brags about his verbal dexterity. His words are his greatest asset, used to manipulate and charm.
*   **The Evolution:** His tongue eventually turns against him. In **Play Crack the Sky**, he warns that *"your tongue is a rudder... but the wrong words will strand you."* Once the guilt of *TDAG* sets in, his ability to speak vanishes entirely. In **Degausser**, he panics: *"I'll never say anything right."* In **Archers**, he actively rejects the bragging of "Tommy Gun," saying he is: *"Throwing away this filthy, silver tongue / If you try run in a maze of your lies... It's too hard to save."*

#### 3. Taking Things Apart (The Origin of Deconstruction)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We're concentrating on falling apart"*
*   **The Connection:** The first time he introduces "falling apart" as a deliberate action or focus. 
*   **The Evolution:** This obsession with deconstruction becomes the defining coping mechanism of his career. In **Jesus Christ**, he fears it will happen to his soul: *"Do I divide and fall apart?"* In **Degausser**, it becomes a manic command: *"Take apart your head / Take apart your friends."* In **Waste**, it finally becomes the cure: *"Take your head apart, free your own heart."*

#### 4. The Meta-Narrative (Mocking the Audience)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I hope this song starts a craze / The kinda song that ignites the airwaves... And these are the words you wish you wrote down / This is the way you wish your voice sounds"*
*   **The Connection:** He actively breaks the fourth wall to mock the consumerism of his own music, daring the listener to blindly consume his sadness.
*   **The Evolution:** This cynical detachment from his art returns in **In The Water** (*Sci-Fi*). Looking back at his success, he feels nothing but guilt: *"Drowning in the praise / Never had a chance to break apart."* The "craze" he hoped to start in 2003 just ended up suffocating him by 2017.

#### 5. The "Rise and Fall" Prophecy

*   **The Lyric:** *"This is the reason you're alone / This is the rise and the fall!"*
*   **The Connection:** He predicts his own inevitable downfall and reinforces the isolation motif ("the reason you're alone").
*   **The Evolution:** The "fall" happens immediately on the next record. The loneliness he weaponizes here becomes his own personal hell in **Jesus Christ** (*"I will die all alone"*). 

#### 6. Fake Authenticity (The TV Motif)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We admit to the truth, we are the best at what we do... And it's all from watching TV / And from speeding up my breathing"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits his "authenticity" is just a performance learned from television and fake panic attacks. 
*   **The Evolution:** The fear of being a fraud haunts him forever. It connects to **Luca** (*"Exposed for a phoney"*), **At the Bottom** (*"I make little lies and then I pull them apart"*), and reaches its devastating conclusion in **Same Logic/Teeth**: *"You're just an actor / But now they caught you / A shade of what you used to be."*

# Track 5: "The Quiet Things That No One Ever Knows" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"The Quiet Things" is the most commercially successful single off *Deja Entendu*, but beneath the catchy hooks, it is a grim narrative about a relationship bleeding out. It establishes the "Medical/Hospital" motif, painting love not as a romance, but as a terminal illness or a fatal injury. It also reinforces the idea of emotional exhaustion—giving up the fight and surrendering to the numbness that will fully take over by the time *Daisy* is released.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Hospital / Medical Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"We saw the western coast, I saw the hospital / Nurse the shoreline like a wound / So keep the blood in your head..."*
*   **The Connection:** The relationship is framed as a medical emergency. The shoreline is a wound, and the priority is keeping blood in the head (staying conscious/alive).
*   **The Evolution:** This clinical, biological approach to trauma expands massively. The bloodletting here connects to **Luca** (*"Drearily bloodletting this bed wetting cosmonaut"*). The medical setting returns in the unreleased track **1996** (*"In a hospital gown and a big bright golden halo"*) and fully manifests in the therapy sessions and surgical failures of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"You're no surgeon / None of your cuts go very straight"*).

#### 2. The Bed / The Empty Hotel

*   **The Lyric:** *"Gave up my body and bed, all for an empty hotel"*
*   **The Connection:** He abandons the intimacy of a shared bed for the sterile isolation of a hotel. The bed is officially dead space.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the corruption of the "Bed" motif. He gives up the bed here, can't fall asleep in it in **Millstone**, violently takes it apart in **Degausser**, and watches it burn in **You Stole**. The "empty hotel" also prefigures the opening lines of **Lit Me Up** (*Sci-Fi*), where the therapy dream takes place in a chaotic hotel (*"The dream is that I'm in some sort of a... And I'm in a hotel"*).

#### 3. Dropping Out / Surrender

*   **The Lyric:** *"Today's the day it gets tired, today's the day we dropped out"*
*   **The Connection:** The exhaustion of trying to save a doomed relationship leads to total surrender. 
*   **The Evolution:** This "dropping out" is the emotional precursor to the total apathy of *Daisy*. The exhaustion here turns into the literal desire to stop fighting the water in **Sink** (*"You want to sink, so I'm gonna let you"*) and the surrender to the tracks in **Noro** (*"Our car's stuck on the train crossing"*).

#### 4. The "Hallelujah" Irony (Religious Burnout)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I lie for only you / And I lie, well Hallelu'"*
*   **The Connection:** He pairs the act of deception with a sarcastic cry of religious praise ("Hallelujah"). 
*   **The Evolution:** This sarcastic use of religious language points to his growing disillusionment with God. It connects to **Jesus Christ** (*"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique... I swear I'll try to nail you back up"*). He realizes his own lies make him unworthy of the grace he begs for, culminating in the complete spiritual burnout of **Limousine**. 

#### 5. The Communication Failure (Wasting Words)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Wasting words on lowercases and capitals... There's nothing new to talk about"*
*   **The Connection:** Communication has completely broken down. They are just arguing over semantics (lowercases and capitals) while the core issues go unspoken (the "quiet things").
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the destruction of his "silver tongue" brag from the previous track. He is already wasting words. This eventually leads to the total communication blackout of **You Won't Know** (*"I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone"*) and **Degausser** (*"I'll never say anything right"*).

#### 6. The Burden of Blame

*   **The Lyric:** *"And though our kids are blessed / Their parents let them shoulder all the blame"*
*   **The Connection:** The sins of the parents being passed down to the children. 
*   **The Evolution:** The concept of carrying blame and guilt is central to *TDAG*. In **Millstone**, he mourns that he *"used to make my parents proud."* By *Sci-Fi*, he focuses on breaking this cycle for his own children in **Could Never Be Heaven**: *"My daughter's shoulders are my shoulders / My son's hands my hands."* He refuses to let his kids shoulder the blame he carried.

# Track 6: "The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
As the title suggests, this song is the ultimate anthem of self-sabotage. It acts as a toxic apology; Jesse admits that he is the villain in the relationship, but rather than fixing his behavior, he wallows in self-pity and offers to martyr himself or disappear. This track is a massive stepping stone between the manipulative arrogance of the first half of *Deja Entendu* and the crushing, internalized self-hatred that will define *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Bed / The Wound (Intimate Cruelty)

*   **The Lyric:** *"It's cold as a tomb, and it's dark in your room / When I sneak to your bed to pour salt in your wounds"*
*   **The Connection:** He actively enters the most intimate space (the bed) specifically to inflict pain and agitate existing injuries.
*   **The Evolution:** The corruption of the Bed motif continues. In **Sic Transit Gloria**, the bed was a place of paralysis. Here, it is a place of emotional torture. This explains the profound guilt he associates with his own bed later in **Millstone** (*"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*) and why he must violently dismantle it in **Degausser** (*"Take apart your bed"*). The "tomb" imagery also perfectly sets up the Grave/Burial motif of later albums.

#### 2. The Stone / The Hardened Heart

*   **The Lyric:** *"A crown of gold, a heart that's harder than stone"*
*   **The Connection:** Following "Tautou" (*"sinkin' like a stone"*), the stone motif returns, this time representing a lack of empathy and emotional deadness.
*   **The Evolution:** Before the stone becomes a literal weapon of drowning around his neck in **Millstone**, it replaces his heart. He realizes his inability to feel or care is his greatest flaw. This emotional numbness peaks in **Mene** (*"We don't feel anything"*) and **Daisy** (*"I'm a sun that doesn't burn hot / I'm a mouth that doesn't smile"*). By *Science Fiction*, he begs to undo this hardening in **Lit Me Up**: *"I want to open up my heart like the ocean."*

#### 3. Self-Erasure / Disappearing

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'll take your pictures all down / Every picture you paint, I will paint myself out"*
*   **The Connection:** His response to guilt is complete withdrawal and self-erasure. If he ruins things, he will just cease to exist in her world.
*   **The Evolution:** The desire to disappear becomes a central coping mechanism. It directly sets up the opening lines of **Luca** (*"When I disappear, do you fear for the sister I took?"*) and the burial in **At the Bottom** (*"And when I lower it down / I'll let you fade away"*). 

#### 4. The Fixed Identity (The Vile Self)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Hope you find out what you are, already know what I am... You can tell me how vile I already know that I am"*
*   **The Connection:** He believes his identity is permanently fixed. He is "vile," and there is no room for redemption or change.
*   **The Evolution:** This deep-seated self-loathing drives the narrative of the next decade. He sees himself as a toxic hazard to everyone around him (**You Won't Know**: *"Your daughters weren't careful / I fear that I am a slippery slope"*). The realization that he cannot easily shed this "vile" identity leads to the frustrated self-surgery in **Same Logic/Teeth**: *"So you thought you'd shed a layer / Maybe try on some new skin... You're just a monster in a costume."*

#### 5. Anatomy & Weather (The Impending Storm)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Pale white, like the skin stretched over your bones... You are the smell before rain / You are the blood in my veins"*
*   **The Connection:** He mixes clinical, skeletal anatomy with meteorological warnings. The "smell before rain" is petrichor—the warning that a storm is about to hit.
*   **The Evolution:** The anatomical obsession continues to grow (blood, bones, skin), leading to his breakdown. But the "smell before rain" is the most prophetic line here. It directly predicts the manic panic of **Degausser** on the next album, where the rain finally arrives: *"The storm is coming, the storm is coming in."* And in **Play Crack the Sky**, that same storm *"broke our second anchor line."*

#### 6. The Crown / Heaven's Bureaucracy

*   **The Lyric:** *"A crown of gold"*
*   **The Connection:** Sarcastic royalty imagery, pointing out that ruling his own toxic kingdom means nothing if his heart is stone. 
*   **The Evolution:** This gold imagery is tied to the afterlife and his fear of judgment. It connects to the unreleased track **1996** (*"And reserve my golden crown / While I am cursed to walk the earth"*) and **Jesus Christ** (*"Do I get the gold chariot?"*). He knows he doesn't deserve the gold.

# Track 7: "Jaws Theme Swimming" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Jaws Theme Swimming" is one of the most vivid, cinematic, and predatory songs on *Deja Entendu*. Blending film-noir imagery with deep-seated paranoia, Jesse sings from the perspective of an observer (or perhaps a participant) stalking the "idle kind." It heavily reinforces the **Car Motif** as a predatory space and introduces the specific imagery of dismemberment ("bits and pieces") that will become crucial to the healing process on *Science Fiction*.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car / Predatory Stalking

*   **The Lyric:** *"In a car outside, we stalk the idle kind"*
*   **The Connection:** The car is not a mode of transport here; it is a blind, a hunting ground, a place to lurk in the dark.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the corruption of the car motif from *Your Favorite Weapon* (where cars were freedom/joyrides). The predatory car directly sets up the very next track, **Me vs. Maradona vs. Elvis**, where he brings a girl "out to the car" to take advantage of her. Eventually, the karma of this behavior catches up, and the car becomes the site of the ultimate tragedy in **Limousine** and his own coffin in **Handcuffs** (*"I'd drive my car off of the bridge"*).

#### 2. The Concrete and Water (The Origin of "Luca")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Concrete and water, she's looking for her daughter / At midnight in torrential downpour"*
*   **The Connection:** This is an incredibly specific and vital combination of words. Concrete and water, set amidst a storm.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the exact recipe for the aquatic execution in **Luca** (*TDAG*). Three years later, Jesse will sing: *"So we fixed you with cement galoshes / And no one can save you now... swimming alone in the lake."* The frantic mother "looking for her daughter" in the downpour also perfectly sets up the warning he issues in **You Won't Know**: *"Your daughters weren't careful / I fear that I am a slippery slope."* 

#### 3. Dismemberment / "Bits and Pieces" (Taking Things Apart)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And everything I said about how messed your head is / Were cut up and left in bits and pieces on the ground"*
*   **The Connection:** Words and mental states are literally cut up and scattered on the floor. 
*   **The Evolution:** This violent deconstruction of the self becomes the central coping mechanism of his career. It traces directly to the manic commands of **Degausser** (*"Take apart your head"*). But most importantly, it finds its resolution 14 years later on *Science Fiction*. In **Waste**, he tells himself he can finally climb out of his grave: *"With the bits of you you managed to save."* The "bits and pieces" scattered on the ground in 2003 are the exact same pieces he gathers up to heal in 2017. 

#### 4. Physical Breakdown / The Leaking Sieve

*   **The Lyric:** *"And we learn as we age / We've learned nothing and my body still aches... Though I love you and my body it leaks like a sieve"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates emotional fatigue with severe, chronic physical pain and literal bodily failure (leaking). 
*   **The Evolution:** The physical breakdown becomes a constant theme. He is "leaking" here, which mirrors the "bloodletting" in **Luca** (*"Drearily bloodletting this bed wetting cosmonaut"*). His body is failing him because of his own actions, leading to the desperate need for physical repair in **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"Broke some bones / And when you glued them back together / You found out you did it wrong"*).

#### 5. The Fatal Leap (Suicidal Ideation)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Take a leap from out the window 'cause it's way too far to go through the door"*
*   **The Connection:** The sudden, intrusive thought that a fatal jump is easier than walking out the door.
*   **The Evolution:** This throwaway line of suicidal ideation becomes the horrifying opening scene of *Daisy*. In **Vices**, the album begins with the repeated lines: *"She said goodbye to the ground... And jumped."* 

#### 6. The Crown & The Throne

*   **The Lyric:** *"And I've seen what happens to the wicked and proud / When they decide to try to take on the throne for the crown"*
*   **The Connection:** A warning against hubris and the desire for power/glory.
*   **The Evolution:** He consistently uses the "crown" to represent an unearned reward or false authority. He gave himself a "crown of gold" in the previous track, and in **1996**, he imagines heaven will *"reserve my golden crown / While I am cursed to walk the earth."* In **Degausser**, he stubbornly insists: *"No matter what they say, I am still the king."* He sees what happens to the wicked and proud, yet he cannot stop acting wicked and proud himself.

# Track 8: "Me vs. Maradona vs. Elvis" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Me vs. Maradona vs. Elvis" is the darkest, most uncomfortably honest track on *Deja Entendu*. It is a slow, methodical confession of a sexual predator. Jesse sings from the perspective of a man (often interpreted as a reflection of his own worst impulses or the rockstar lifestyle) who uses alcohol, fake sympathy, and the cold weather to manipulate intoxicated women. It is the absolute peak of the "Arrogance/Manipulation" arc, setting up the necessary karmic destruction that will shatter him on *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car as a Trap

*   **The Lyric:** *"But five or six, bring you out to the car"*
*   **The Connection:** Following "Jaws Theme Swimming" (*"In a car outside, we stalk the idle kind"*), the car is used explicitly as a predatory space to isolate a victim.
*   **The Evolution:** This manipulative use of the vehicle directly predicts his own karmic punishment. In **Handcuffs** (*TDAG*), he turns the car into a cage for himself and his victim: *"I'd throw you down in the back seat / As if you'd committed a terrible crime... I'd drive my car off of the bridge."* He goes from using the car for manipulation to using it for mutual destruction. 

#### 2. The Weaponized Tongue (Lying Awake)

*   **The Lyric:** *"My tongue will taste the gin and malicious intent... I will lie awake / And lie for fun and fake the way I hold you / Let you fall for every empty word I say"*
*   **The Connection:** The dual meaning of "lie" (laying in bed vs. telling falsehoods). His tongue, which he bragged about in "Okay I Believe You," is used purely for deception and malice.
*   **The Evolution:** The guilt of these exact lies will completely destroy his ability to communicate later. On the next album, he panics in **Degausser**: *"I'm on my own, I'll never say anything right."* In **Jesus Christ**, he admits this behavior makes him unworthy of salvation: *"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique... I swear I'll try to nail you back up."*

#### 3. The Stone Heart (Biblical Hypocrisy)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Who will cast the first stone? / You can sin or spend the night all alone / Brass buttons on your coat hold the cold / In the shape of a heart that they cut out of stone"*
*   **The Connection:** He references Jesus's teaching ("let him who is without sin cast the first stone") to ironically justify his own predatory behavior. Furthermore, the girl's heart is described as being "cut out of stone."
*   **The Evolution:** The stone imagery is inescapable. In *The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot*, his *own* heart was "harder than stone." By *TDAG*, the stone is no longer a heart—it is an execution device. **Millstone**: *"I'm my own stone around my neck."* He dared people to cast the first stone here, and he ended up casting it on himself.

#### 4. The Anatomy of Deconstruction (Tearing Apart)

*   **The Lyric:** *"If you let me have my way, I swear I'll tear you apart"*
*   **The Connection:** He promises absolute emotional and psychological destruction to his partner. He is the active agent of deconstruction.
*   **The Evolution:** The karma of this threat is brutal. On *TDAG*, he is no longer tearing women apart; he is tearing *himself* apart. The threat here becomes the frantic command of **Degausser**: *"Take apart your head / Take apart your bed / Take apart your friends."* By **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*), he actually does it to himself: *"So you decide to make incisions at your home while you're alone."*

#### 5. Alcohol as a Tool vs. Poison

*   **The Lyric:** *"With one or two, I get used to the room... Number nine with my head on the bar... You're a drunk and you're scared"*
*   **The Connection:** Alcohol is used strategically to lower inhibitions and trap the girl. He is "a sober straight face" while she is "a drunk and scared." 
*   **The Evolution:** Alcohol transitions from a manipulative tool to a fatal poison. The drinking here sets the stage for the drunk driver in **Limousine** (*"He's drinking up, he's all-American, and he'll drive"*), which ends in the death of a child. After *TDAG*, alcohol/drugs are no longer used on others; they are used to numb himself in **Waste** (*"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down"*). 

#### 6. The Bedroom / Isolation Fear

*   **The Lyric:** *"You can sin or spend the night all alone"*
*   **The Connection:** He weaponizes the fear of isolation. He knows she doesn't want to be alone, so she will agree to his terms.
*   **The Evolution:** This is maximum projection. *He* is the one terrified of spending the night alone. This weaponized threat becomes his own existential dread in **Jesus Christ**: *"But with nobody in your bed / The night's hard to get through / And I will die all alone."*

# Track 9: "Guernica" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
Named after Pablo Picasso's famous painting of a brutal bombing during the Spanish Civil War, "Guernica" is a desperate, frantic song about Jesse Lacey's grandfather battling cancer. It is a massive tonal shift from the manipulative arrogance of the previous track. Here, Jesse is completely powerless. He wants to save a loved one but is confronted with the failure of the human body and the limits of his own ability. This track heavily establishes the biological/medical motif and the crushing weight of family legacy.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Anatomy Motif (Cellular Level)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Please use my body while I sleep / My lungs are fresh and yours to keep... If I could I would shrink myself / Sink through your skin to your blood cells / Remove whatever makes you hurt"*
*   **The Connection:** He offers up his own biological parts (lungs) and imagines physically entering a bloodstream to fight a disease at the cellular level.
*   **The Evolution:** This introduces the hyper-specific biological imagery that dominates the band's later work. His offer of fresh lungs contrasts with the suffocating water in his lungs in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"I need you like water in my lungs"*). The idea of existing at a cellular level returns in **Bought a Bride** (*Daisy*): *"Cars are little blood cells / We are oxygen."* The frustrated desire to perform surgery here eventually turns into the literal self-mutilation of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"You're no surgeon / None of your cuts go very straight"*).

#### 2. The Machine / The Battery

*   **The Lyric:** *"Is this the way a toy feels when its batteries run dry? / I am the watch you always wear but you forget to wind"*
*   **The Connection:** He compares the failing human body to a machine running out of power, highlighting the cold, mechanical nature of death.
*   **The Evolution:** The "machine" metaphor expands significantly. In **Jesus Christ**, the entire system of organized religion and human cruelty is a machine: *"We turn out hate in factories / Yeah, we all got wood and nails / And we sleep inside of this machine."* In **Not The Sun**, he begs a lover to *"Turn up your love like some cold machine."* The "batteries running dry" also predicts the total energetic depletion of **Degausser** (*"I've lost my charge, I've been degaussed"*).

#### 3. The Sleeping / Bedside Vigil

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm there to monitor your breathing / I will watch you while you're sleeping"*
*   **The Connection:** Instead of the bed being a place of predatory behavior (as in the previous track), it is a place of anxious medical surveillance. He is terrified the person will stop breathing while asleep.
*   **The Evolution:** This completely destroys his relationship with sleep. Because sleep is associated with death or trauma, he develops the severe insomnia detailed in **Degausser** (*"Goodbye to sleep / I think this staying up is exactly what I need"*). By *Daisy*, the anxiety over sleeping affects everyone he knows in **Noro**: *"Why doesn't anyone I know sleep? / Are they all just scared of their dreams?"*

#### 4. Distance and Isolation (Half a World Away)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Nobody plans to be half a world away at times like these / So I sat alone and waited out the night"*
*   **The Connection:** He is trapped on tour, physically separated from his dying family member. He is sitting alone in the dark.
*   **The Evolution:** This specific type of isolation (waiting in the dark for bad news) returns in the unreleased track **Yeah (Sowing Season Demo)**, where he describes waiting for a signal that never comes: *"Listening at night / Waiting for a sound to come up the stairs... They don't send you letters / But you're waiting for them."* It reinforces the ultimate fear of **Jesus Christ**: *"I will die all alone."*

#### 5. The "Cure"

*   **The Lyric:** *"But I am too weak to be your cure"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits his total inadequacy when faced with real trauma. He cannot save the person he loves.
*   **The Evolution:** The obsession with "cures" and "sickness" persists. In **Gasoline** (*Daisy*), he recognizes the spiritual rot in himself: *"So there is a sickness that's going 'round / But no one's got a vaccine."* In **Can't Get It Out** (*Sci-Fi*), he looks at his audience and wonders: *"Were you one of the cured kids?"*

***

`[Anatomy/Lungs & Blood]` `[The Machine/Batteries]` `[The Bed/Insomnia]` `[Isolation/Waiting]` `[Medical/The Cure]`

# Track 10: "Good to Know That If I Ever Need Attention All I Have to Do is Die" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
With an absurdly long title reminiscent of *Your Favorite Weapon*, this track is a blistering, aggressive return to the "Weaponized Songwriting" motif. Jesse lashes out at his critics, the music scene, and the people who exploit his art for their own gain. However, while he sounds victorious here—claiming he will cut the nooses tied for him—this song sets up the violent execution imagery (gallows, wolves, slit throats) that will ultimately be used against him on the next two albums. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Execution Motif (The Gallows & The Noose)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We burn the gallows they erect / And cut the nooses they tie for our necks"*
*   **The Connection:** He frames himself as a defiant survivor. The industry/critics build gallows for him, but he burns them down and cuts the rope. 
*   **The Evolution:** This defiance completely vanishes by the next album. In *The Devil and God*, he is no longer fighting the executioner; he is actively calling for him. In **You Won't Know**, he sings: *"Hey hey hey, Mr. Hangman / Go get your rope."* By **Millstone**, the noose has been replaced by a much heavier weight: *"I'm my own stone around my neck."* He cut the noose in 2003 only to hang himself in 2006.

#### 2. The Meta-Narrative (The Pen and the Page)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Am I correct to defend the fist that holds this pen? / Its ink that lies / The pen, the page, the paper"*
*   **The Connection:** Acknowledging the power—and the inherent deception—of his own songwriting. He admits his ink "lies."
*   **The Evolution:** The anxiety over his own lying becomes a massive spiritual crisis. In **Jesus Christ**, he admits: *"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique."* He realizes that if his art is built on lies, he cannot be saved. This leads to the desperate desire to abandon songwriting altogether in **Bought a Bride** (*Daisy*): *"If somehow I was new and everything was unsaid / I'd go and buy a hammer, never sing again."*

#### 3. Communication Breakdown (Comatose but Audible)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You constantly make it impossible to make conversation / We're comatose but audible"*
*   **The Connection:** Despite being a singer (audible), he feels completely disconnected and unable to communicate effectively (comatose). 
*   **The Evolution:** This introduces the "paralyzed speech" motif. It predicts the flooded radio in **Play Crack the Sky** and the useless telephone in **You Won't Know**. Ultimately, the inability to converse here becomes the total linguistic failure of **Degausser**: *"I'll never say anything right."*

#### 4. The Predator Motif (Wolves & Sheep)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We prey as wolves among the sheep / And slit the necks of soldiers while they sleep"*
*   **The Connection:** He embraces the role of the villain/predator, infiltrating the scene to destroy it from the inside. 
*   **The Evolution:** The "wolf" imagery returns in **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*), but the perspective has flipped. Jesse sings from the perspective of a paranoid father protecting his son from predators: *"I'd hold him down on the Tompkins Lake / Before I ever let him go to the wolves."* Furthermore, the boast about slitting throats connects directly to the anatomical deconstruction (cutting/incisions) that dominates later albums. 

#### 5. Holding the Past (Refusal to Forgive)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Once said, always said / I will hold the past over your head... I am hellbent on extracting all of my revenge"*
*   **The Connection:** He refuses to let go of grudges. He is determined to punish people for their past mistakes.
*   **The Evolution:** This exact mindset is what condemns him in the "Bureaucracy of Heaven" arc. Because he refuses to forgive others here, he realizes he cannot ask God for forgiveness later. By **You Won't Know**, he knows that when he arrives in heaven, *"They'll be completely out of their forgiveness supplies."* It takes until **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*) for him to finally let the past go: *"It's going to feel so good to let it go / It's all in your head, your race is run."*

# Track 11: "Play Crack the Sky" (*Deja Entendu*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
As the acoustic closer to *Deja Entendu*, "Play Crack the Sky" is the absolute Rosetta Stone for Brand New's lyrical universe. Using the real-life 1951 sinking of the *Pelican* off Montauk Point as an allegory for a doomed relationship, Jesse Lacey establishes the nautical, weather, and isolation motifs that will dominate *The Devil and God*, *Daisy*, and *Science Fiction*. It is a masterpiece of fatalism, marking the exact moment the band shifts from teenage angst to existential dread.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Shipwreck (Sinking in Sight of Land)

*   **The Lyric:** *"To the port I see the lighthouse... The morning finds our bodies washed up thirty miles west"*
*   **The Connection:** The tragedy of this specific shipwreck is that it happened so close to the shore. They could see the lighthouse, but they still drowned. 
*   **The Evolution:** This specific imagery is the direct precursor to **Jesus Christ** (*TDAG*), where Jesse summarizes his entire spiritual crisis with the line: *"And this ship went down in sight of land."* The nautical doom continues in **Luca** (*"fixed you with cement galoshes"*) and **At the Bottom** (*"at the bottom you'll find all my friends / they don't swim 'cause they're all dead"*). 

#### 2. The Water / Drowning Motif (The Transition to "Float On")

*   **The Lyric:** *"I need you like water in my lungs"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates his love/need for someone to the very thing that is actively killing him. He is choosing to drown.
*   **The Evolution:** This suffocating relationship with water persists for 14 years. In **Sink** (*Daisy*), he finally gives up fighting it: *"You want to sink, so I'm gonna let you."* The resolution to this exact lyric finally arrives in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he decides to stop breathing the water in and just let it carry him: *"Well, you could turn to water and we'd all float on."* 

#### 3. The Communication Failure (The Flooded Radio & The Tongue)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Flooded the engines and radio... Your tongue is a rudder / It steers the whole ship / Sends your words past your lips... But the wrong words will strand you"*
*   **The Connection:** The dual failure of communication. First, the mechanical failure (the flooded radio means the SOS call fails). Second, the biological failure (his tongue steers his life, and his bad words have stranded him). 
*   **The Evolution:** The flooded radio evolves into the useless telephone in **You Won't Know** (*"I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone"*). The warning about the "tongue" comes true in **Degausser**, where his inability to steer his life culminates in the panic: *"I'll never say anything right."* By **Archers**, he wants to cut it out entirely: *"Throwing away this filthy, silver tongue."*

#### 4. The Storm

*   **The Lyric:** *"When the storm broke our second anchor line... (after the storm)"*
*   **The Connection:** The storm is the external, overwhelming force that destroys the ship. 
*   **The Evolution:** By the next album, the storm is no longer an external weather event; it is an internal psychological breakdown. The storm that breaks the ship here is the exact same storm approaching in **Degausser**: *"The storm is coming, the storm is coming in."* 

#### 5. The "Alone" Motif (The Ultimate Reversal)

*   **The Lyric:** *(Overlapping Outro)* *"You know that you are not alone"*
*   **The Connection:** Sung in the background as the song fades out, it is a ghostly comfort to someone drowning alongside him.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the most profound thematic arc in the band's history. 
    *   *Act I (Deja):* "You are not alone" (Shared doom).
    *   *Act II (TDAG):* He loses this comfort in **Jesus Christ**: *"I will die all alone."* 
    *   *Act III (Daisy):* He is isolated in **At the Bottom**: *"I'm alone now in my bed."* 
    *   *Act IV (Sci-Fi):* He reclaims the *Play Crack the Sky* lyric, putting it front and center as a mantra of survival in **Waste**: *"You are not alone, you are not alone... you get to reset."* 

#### 6. The "End"

*   **The Lyric:** *(Overlapping Outro)* *"This is the end... This story's old but it goes on and on / Until we disappear"*
*   **The Connection:** The fatalistic acceptance of death. 
*   **The Evolution:** This directly sets up the finale of the band's career. In **Waste**, he echoes this exact finality: *"This is the last one."* In **Desert**, he hears the apocalypse: *"Last night I heard a voice that said, 'This is the end.'"*

***

`[Water/Sinking/Shipwreck]` `[Communication/Radio & Tongue]` `[Weather/The Storm]` `[Isolation/Not Alone]` `[Fatalism/The End]`

# Track 1: "Sowing Season (Yeah)" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Sowing Season (Yeah)" is the explosive, brooding opening to *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*. It immediately shatters the arrogant, fast-talking persona of *Deja Entendu*. The song is about trying to recover from profound loss and the realization that his youth is over. It officially grounds the Brand New universe—shifting the band's imagery away from vast oceans and into the cold, hard dirt. It also introduces the literal, real-world horror of drunk driving that will haunt the rest of the album.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash Motif (The Tragic Reality)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Was losing all my friends / Was losing them to drinking and to driving"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the sobering reality check of the Brand New universe. In *Your Favorite Weapon*, he wished drunk driving crashes on his enemies (*Seventy Times 7*: "Have another drink and drive yourself home..."). Here, that exact curse becomes a real-life horror. He is losing the people he loves to the exact thing he used to joke about.
*   **The Evolution:** This line single-handedly sets up the emotional core of the album: **Limousine**. The "drinking and driving" mentioned here manifests as the "all-American" drunk driver who kills a child in track 5. The car crash motif is no longer a metaphor; it is a source of inescapable, crushing guilt that will last all the way until **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*).

#### 2. The Earth / Burial Motif (The Cold Ground)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Time to get the seeds into the cold ground... Before you put my body in the cold ground"*
*   **The Connection:** He prepares for death and burial. This introduces dirt, soil, and the grave to the band's visual lexicon.
*   **The Evolution:** This burying imagery becomes an obsession on the next album, *Daisy*. In **At the Bottom**, he digs the grave (*"I carry this box to the proper place... turned to dirt and dust"*). In the title track **Daisy**, he repeats the exact imagery from *Sowing Season*: *"If we take all these things and we bury them fast / And we pray that they turn to seeds, to roots and then grass."* It takes until **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*) for him to finally *"climb on up out of your grave."*

#### 3. The Brick / Sabotage Motif

*   **The Lyric:** *"Take all that you have / And turn it into something you would miss if / Somebody threw that brick, shattered all your plans"*
*   **The Connection:** A sudden, violent destruction of everything he has built, currently attributed to an external force ("somebody").
*   **The Evolution:** As his guilt complex deepens, he realizes *he* is the one throwing the bricks. In **At the Bottom** (*Daisy*), he admits to his own self-sabotage: *"I stole bricks from the dam almost everyday / Now I'm drowning in the flood I made."* The brick that shatters his plans in 2006 is thrown by his own hand in 2009.

#### 4. Total Social Isolation (Stripping Titles)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm not your friend / I'm not your lover / I'm not your family"*
*   **The Connection:** He systematically strips away every social label that connects him to other human beings. He is alienating himself completely.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the "Die Alone" prophecy from *Deja Entendu*. Because he severs these ties here, he faces the brutal social collapse of the next track, **Millstone** (*"I was the glue that kept my friends together / Now they don't talk"*). In **Not The Sun**, the person he pushed away throws this back in his face: *"Say you're my friend, but why won't you be my family?"* He finally repairs these titles in **Could Never Be Heaven** (*Sci-Fi*): *"My son's hands my hands / My wife's heart my own heart."*

#### 5. "Yeah!" (The Loss of the Silver Tongue)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Yeah! Yeah! Yeah, yeah!"*
*   **The Connection:** In *Deja Entendu*, Jesse bragged about his vocabulary ("wasting words on lowercases and capitals"). Here, the choruses are stripped of all articulate thought. He just screams "Yeah!" repeatedly over exploding guitars.
*   **The Evolution:** His "silver tongue" has officially failed him. Words are no longer enough to express his pain. This leads directly into the communication blackouts of **Degausser** (*"I'll never say anything right"*) and **You Won't Know** (*"I can't use the telephone"*).

# Track 2: "Millstone" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Millstone" is a massive pillar in the Brand New lyrical universe. If *Deja Entendu* was about the fear of ruining things, "Millstone" is the grim realization that you already have. The song is a tragic autopsy of Jesse’s loss of innocence. It officially connects the Car Crash, Water/Sinking, and Silence of God motifs into one suffocating narrative of unredeemable guilt. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Heavy Weight & The Execution (The Title)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm my own stone around my neck"*
*   **The Connection:** The title "Millstone" references Matthew 18:6: *"...it would be better for him to have a heavy millstone hung around his neck, and to be drowned in the depth of the sea."* He is acknowledging that he deserves to be executed for his sins, and he is carrying out the execution himself.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the literal realization of the opening line of *Deja Entendu* (**Tautou**: *"I'm sinkin' like a stone in the sea"*). The stone imagery continues into *Daisy* (**In a Jar**: *"Stones in your eyes / Stones in your mouth"*). It takes 11 years for him to finally remove the stone in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I'm hoping that in time, you can lay down / All this weight you've been carrying around."*

#### 2. The Shipwreck (Sinking)

*   **The Lyric:** *"This ship of fools I'm on will sink"*
*   **The Connection:** He knows the trajectory of his life/relationships is doomed because the people involved (including himself) are "fools."
*   **The Evolution:** This completes the trilogy of the Shipwreck arc. 
    *   *Play Crack the Sky:* The ship is out of control in a storm.
    *   *Millstone:* The pessimistic certainty that the ship *will* sink.
    *   *Jesus Christ:* The tragedy strikes: *"And this ship went down in sight of land."*

#### 3. The Car Crash (The Fatal Mistake)

*   **The Lyric:** *"They never hit their brakes / There was no time to see / He just ran out in the street... He sure as hell paid for that mistake"*
*   **The Connection:** A sudden, violent pedestrian accident. It is an externalization of his sudden, life-ruining guilt.
*   **The Evolution:** The car crash motif continues to build toward the literal tragedy of **Limousine**. The accident here is a "mistake." But by **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), the crash is no longer an accident; *"laying in the road"* is a *"shoe-in for the crash of the day,"* meaning he had accepted this violent destruction as a daily routine.

#### 4. The Silence of God (The Loss of Faith)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I used to pray like God was listening"*
*   **The Connection:** The breaking point of his faith. In his youth, he believed he was heard. Now, he knows he is speaking to an empty room.
*   **The Evolution:** This introduces the "Bureaucracy of God" motif that dominates the album. Because God stops listening here, Jesse is forced to beg for a sign in **Jesus Christ**. In **Limousine**, Jesus finally shows up but says *"Never again."* In **You Won't Know**, heaven is out of *"forgiveness supplies."* Finally, in **Waste**, he accepts the reality of "Millstone" and admits: *"We get no reply."*

#### 5. The Burning Example (Loss of the "Bright")

*   **The Lyric:** *"I used to be such a burning example / I used to be so original"*
*   **The Connection:** He mourns his lost purity. The "burning" represents his soul and his potential.
*   **The Evolution:** He realizes his fire is going out. In **Jesus Christ**, he admits his *"bright is too slight to hold back all my dark."* In **Degausser**, he notes that he *"burns bright but runs out."* By **You Won't Know**, he is terrified: *"I'm scared I won't burn that bright."* 

#### 6. The Bed (The Insomnia of Guilt)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Now I've made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*
*   **The Connection:** The idiom "you made your bed, now lie in it" is made literal. His guilt causes severe insomnia.
*   **The Evolution:** The bed is now permanently associated with trauma and wakefulness. This leads directly to the panic of **Degausser** (*"Goodbye to sleep... take apart your bed"*), the total isolation of **At the Bottom** (*"I'm alone now in my bed"*), and the literal flames of **You Stole** (*"And now the flames are burning me in my bed"*). 

#### 7. The Glue (Social Collapse)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I was the glue that kept my friends together / Now they don't talk and we don't go out"*
*   **The Connection:** Following the alienation of *Sowing Season* ("I'm not your friend"), his social circle completely collapses because he failed to hold it together.
*   **The Evolution:** The concept of "glue" returns in a much darker way in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*). Instead of holding friends together, the glue represents his inescapable bond with his own past/trauma: *"But you and I are stuck like glue / And that's the God damn truth."*

# Track 3: "Jesus Christ" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Jesus Christ" is the existential anchor of the Brand New discography. It is a slow, methodical plea to a Savior that Jesse is not sure he believes in, and is certain he doesn't deserve. If *Millstone* is the realization of guilt, *Jesus Christ* is the paralyzing fear of the consequences. It directly addresses the "Die Alone" prophecy, the sinking of the ship, the exhaustion of Grace, and the mechanical, factory-like nature of human cruelty. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The "Die Alone" Prophecy (The Ultimate Terror)

*   **The Lyric:** *"But with nobody in your bed / The night's hard to get through / And I will die all alone / And when I arrive I won't know anyone"*
*   **The Connection:** This is the realization of the cynical prophecy he made in *Deja Entendu* (**I Will Play My Game...**: *"Everyone who lives will someday die and die alone"*). It is no longer a cool, cynical lyric; it is his greatest waking nightmare.
*   **The Evolution:** This fear haunts him through *Daisy* (**At the Bottom**: *"Some die looking for a hand to hold"*). The resolution to this exact panic is the emotional climax of *Science Fiction*. In **Waste**, he repeatedly reassures his younger self: *"You are not alone, you are not alone."* He didn't die alone. 

#### 2. The Shipwreck (Sinking in Sight of Land)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And this ship went down in sight of land"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate tragedy. The ship didn't just sink; it sank right before reaching safety. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the direct sequel to **Play Crack the Sky** (*"To the port I see the lighthouse"*). He could see the land, but he still went down. He accepts this aquatic doom until **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he decides to stop fighting the water: *"Well, you could turn to water and we'd all float on."*

#### 3. The Bureaucracy of Heaven (The Gold Chariot & The Sign)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Do I get the gold chariot? / Do I float through the ceiling? ... And at the gates does Thomas ask to see my hands? ... So do you think that we could work out a sign?"*
*   **The Connection:** He treats the afterlife and divine forgiveness like a stressful administrative process. He wants a sign or a contract to guarantee he is safe.
*   **The Evolution:** Because God stopped listening in **Millstone**, Jesse has to beg for a sign here. He never gets it. In **Limousine**, Jesus tells him *"Never again."* In **You Won't Know**, heaven is out of *"forgiveness supplies."* Finally, in **Waste**, he accepts that *"we get no reply."* The bureaucratic system of heaven failed him.

#### 4. The "Bright" vs. The "Dark"

*   **The Lyric:** *"'Cause my bright is too slight to hold back all my dark"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits his soul (the bright) is fundamentally weaker than his sins/depression (the dark).
*   **The Evolution:** This explains the loss of his "fire" in **Millstone** (*"I used to be such a burning example"*). It predicts the panic of **Degausser** (*"You burn bright but you're running"*) and **You Won't Know** (*"Even though I'm scared I won't burn that bright"*). 

#### 5. Anatomy of Deconstruction (Divide and Fall Apart)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Do I divide and fall apart?"*
*   **The Connection:** He fears that his soul or identity will literally break into pieces upon death.
*   **The Evolution:** This fear of falling apart becomes an obsessive command in the very next track, **Degausser** (*"Take apart your head"*). It culminates in the botched self-surgery of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"So you decide to make incisions... glued them back together"*). In **Waste**, taking himself apart finally becomes an act of healing rather than a punishment.

#### 6. Wood, Nails, and The Machine

*   **The Lyric:** *"We all got wood and nails / We turn out hate in factories / Yeah, we sleep inside of this machine"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates human nature (the wood and nails used to crucify Christ) to an industrial, mechanical factory of hate. 
*   **The Evolution:** The "machine" metaphor returns in **Not The Sun** (*"Turn up your love like some cold machine"*) and **451** (*"Fired up my fear machine"*). It represents the cold, unfeeling systems that humans build to hurt each other, reinforcing the apathy of the world around him.

#### 7. The Lying Technique

*   **The Lyric:** *"But I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique / I know you think that I'm someone you can trust / But I'm scared I'll get scared and I swear I'll try to nail you back up"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits that the "silver tongue" he bragged about in *Deja Entendu* makes him untrustworthy, even to Jesus. If pushed, he will betray his Savior to save himself.
*   **The Evolution:** Because his words are built on lies, he eventually loses the ability to speak truthfully at all. This leads to the communication blackout in **Degausser** (*"I'll never say anything right"*) and **Archers** (*"Throwing away this filthy, silver tongue"*).

# Track 4: "Degausser" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Degausser" is the chaotic, manic centerpiece of the album. A "degausser" is a machine used to erase data from magnetic tape or hard drives. The entire song is the sound of Jesse trying to violently wipe his own brain clean. It is a frantic panic attack about insomnia, failing the "test" of life, and the complete breakdown of his ability to communicate. It solidifies the "Deconstruction" and "Anatomy" motifs that will haunt the rest of his career.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Anatomy of Breakdown (Take Apart)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Take apart your bed / Take apart the counting... Take apart your head... Take apart your friends... Take apart your demons"*
*   **The Connection:** Unable to handle his guilt, his only coping mechanism is to violently dismantle every aspect of his life. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the realization of the fear in **Jesus Christ** (*"Do I divide and fall apart?"*). The obsession with taking things apart culminates in the literal self-surgery of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*): *"So you decide to make incisions... glued them back together, you found out you did it wrong."* However, in **Waste**, "taking apart" finally becomes the cure: *"Take your head apart, free your own heart."*

#### 2. The Degausser (Erasure vs. The Reset)

*   **The Lyric:** *(Background Vocals)* *"I've lost my charge, I've been degaussed"*
*   **The Connection:** He wants to be magnetically wiped clean, erasing all his memories and sins.
*   **The Evolution:** This painful desire for erasure is finally achieved in a healthy way 11 years later. In **Waste**, he reclaims the concept of wiping the tape clean: *"You are not alone, you get to reset."* 

#### 3. Communication Breakdown (Total Failure)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'll never get anything right... I'm on my own, I'll never say anything right"*
*   **The Connection:** The "silver tongue" of *Deja Entendu* is dead. Because he "honed his lying technique" in **Jesus Christ**, his ability to communicate truthfully has completely failed.
*   **The Evolution:** This linguistic failure leads directly to **Archers**, where he decides to stop speaking entirely: *"Throwing away this filthy, silver tongue."* It also predicts the dead telephone in **You Won't Know** (*"I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone"*). 

#### 4. Sleep Deprivation / The Bed

*   **The Lyric:** *"Goodbye to sleep / I think this staying up is exactly what I need / Well, take apart your bed... Take me, take me back to your bed"*
*   **The Connection:** Guilt causes severe insomnia. He destroys his own bed, then begs to be taken back to someone else's.
*   **The Evolution:** The "dead bedroom" from *YFW* and the guilt of **Millstone** (*"Can't fall asleep in it"*) erupt into total sleeplessness. By *Daisy*, this insomnia infects everyone he knows in **Noro**: *"Why doesn't anyone I know sleep?"* 

#### 5. The Bureaucracy of Heaven (The Record Keeper)

*   **The Lyric:** *"But life is a test and I get bad marks / Now some saint got the job of writing down my sins"*
*   **The Connection:** Heaven is a bureaucracy, and he is failing the audit. God isn't a loving father; he's an administrative clerk writing down failures.
*   **The Evolution:** Because of this ledger of sins, he knows he won't be saved. This leads directly to **Limousine** (Jesus says *"Never again"*) and **You Won't Know** (*"They'll be completely out of their forgiveness supplies"*). He never gets a good grade, which is why in **Waste**, he accepts that *"we get no reply."*

#### 6. Chemical vs. Biological (Alkaline & Rust)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I fell asleep in the alkaline... I've been on pause, I'm shaking off the rust"*
*   **The Connection:** He compares himself to an old, corroded battery (alkaline/rust). He is a machine breaking down.
*   **The Evolution:** This "machine/battery" metaphor started in **Guernica** (*"Is this the way a toy feels when its batteries run dry?"*). The rusted, chemical state predicts the drug use and exhaustion of **Waste** (*"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down"*). 

#### 7. The Storm Arrives

*   **The Lyric:** *"The storm is coming, the storm is coming in"*
*   **The Connection:** The meteorological warning of a massive breakdown. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the exact storm that sank the ship in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"When the storm broke our second anchor line"*). The warning from **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot** (*"You are the smell before rain"*) has come true. The storm is here.

# Track 5: "Limousine" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Limousine" is the devastating emotional core of the Brand New discography. Based on the real-life 2005 tragedy of seven-year-old Katie Flynn, who was decapitated by a drunk driver while returning from a wedding on Long Island, the song forces Jesse to confront the horrific reality of his own metaphors. The car crashes and "all-American" drinking he joked about on *Your Favorite Weapon* are no longer lyrics; they are a nightmare. This song officially ends his belief in divine intervention and establishes the crushing, physical weight of guilt.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash (The Realization of the Curse)

*   **The Lyric:** *"He's drinking up, he's all-American / And he'll drive / He's volunteered with grace to end your life"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate, tragic collision of his earlier motifs. The "drinking and driving" mentioned in **Sowing Season** is carried out here.
*   **The Evolution:** In **Seventy Times 7** (*YFW*), Jesse weaponized this exact scenario: *"Have another drink and drive yourself home... head goes through the windshield."* The reality of this event destroys him. The road is forever cursed. This leads directly to **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he accepts his karmic punishment: *"Laying in the road / Was a shoe-in for the crash of the day / And we're never going to walk away."*

#### 2. The Revocation of Grace (Jesus Says No)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And in the choir, I saw our sad Messiah / He was bored and tired of my laments / Said, 'I died for you one time, but never again'"*
*   **The Connection:** The absolute breaking point of his faith. He looks to Jesus for comfort, but Jesus revokes his sacrifice.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the climax of the "Bureaucracy of Heaven" motif. Because God stopped listening in **Millstone**, and Jesse failed the test in **Degausser**, Jesus finally speaks here just to say the grace is gone. This is why heaven is out of *"forgiveness supplies"* in **You Won't Know**. It perfectly explains his exhausted acceptance in **Waste**: *"Always begging for some grace / When there's no time, we get no reply."*

#### 3. Rejecting the Reply

*   **The Lyric:** *"But do me a favor, baby, don't reply / 'Cause I can dish it out / But I can't take it"*
*   **The Connection:** A brilliant, tragic reversal. Consumed by guilt, he *begs* not to get an answer from the universe. He admits he is a coward.
*   **The Evolution:** In almost every other song, Jesse is desperate for a reply (**Jesus Christ**: *"Work out a sign"* / **Degausser**: *"Waiting for a reply"*). But here, the reality is too horrific. This directly sets up **Waste**: *"We get no reply."*

#### 4. The Nautical Motif (Even in a Car Crash)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You tiny boat, with oars... The world tilts back and pours and pours... You tidal wave"*
*   **The Connection:** Even when describing a car crash on a highway, Jesse defaults to nautical imagery. The dying child is a "tiny boat," and the grief is a "tidal wave."
*   **The Evolution:** This proves that the "Water/Drowning" motif is his fundamental language for tragedy. It connects the fatal car crash here to the fatal shipwreck in **Play Crack the Sky** and the concrete drowning in **Luca**. 

#### 5. The Crushing Guilt vs. The Grave

*   **The Lyric:** *"But can I get myself out from underneath / This guilt that will crush me? ... We'll never have to rot together underneath the dirt"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates emotional guilt to the physical weight of dirt and the grave. 
*   **The Evolution:** He establishes the "dirt/grave" motif that dominates *Daisy*. In **At the Bottom**, he starts *"digging that hole... turned to dirt and dust."* The resolution to this exact lyric finally arrives in **Waste**. *Can I get myself out from underneath?* Yes: *"I'm hoping that in time, you can lay down / All this weight... To climb on up out of your grave."*

#### 6. The "All-American" Poison

*   **The Lyric:** *"He's drinking up, he's all-American"*
*   **The Connection:** Being "American" is framed as a toxic identity associated with blind, reckless consumption that ruins lives.
*   **The Evolution:** This specific branding of guilt returns 11 years later. In **Waste**, when looking back at his own sins and self-destructive habits, he sings: *"You and I repent of our sins / Yeah, we feel so American."* The drunk driver was American; Jesse feels American. 

#### 7. The Explosion

*   **The Lyric:** *"You explode / You firefly"*
*   **The Connection:** The literal explosion of the vehicle impact.
*   **The Evolution:** In **The Shower Scene** (*YFW*), he wished for this: *"I hope she's caught in the explosion."* The karma of that lyric is realized here.

# Track 6: "You Won't Know" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
If "Limousine" is the tragedy, "You Won't Know" is the manic, guilt-ridden aftermath. It is the sound of someone realizing they are a toxic hazard to the people they love and actively choosing to disappear. Jesse calls for his own execution, confirms that heaven is out of grace, and completely loses the ability to communicate with the outside world. It serves as the bridge between the sinking of *Deja Entendu* and the exhausted floating of *Science Fiction*.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. Sinking vs. Floating (The Pivot)

*   **The Lyric:** *"They can fire everything they've got / And when you think I'm sunk I will float on and on"*
*   **The Connection:** Amidst the doom, this is a defiant, manic insistence that he will survive the water.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the crucial bridge in the Nautical arc. In **Play Crack the Sky** and **Millstone**, he is actively sinking. In **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), he peacefully accepts the water: *"You could turn to water and we'd all float on."* "You Won't Know" is the violent transition between the two—he refuses to sink, stubbornly insisting he will "float on."

#### 2. The Bureaucracy of Heaven (The Empty Supply)

*   **The Lyric:** *"On the day that I show up / They'll be completely out / Of their forgiveness supplies"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate confirmation of his spiritual doom. God is just an administrator, and the warehouse is empty.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the direct result of the previous track. Because Jesus said *"Never again"* in **Limousine**, the forgiveness supplies are officially depleted here. This perfectly sets up **Waste**: *"Always begging for some grace / When there's no time, we get no reply."* There is no point in begging if the supplies are gone.

#### 3. The Telephone / Communication Blackout

*   **The Lyric:** *"And I can't use the telephone / To tell you that I'm dead and gone"*
*   **The Connection:** He is unable to communicate his fate or his apologies across the distance of death (or emotional isolation).
*   **The Evolution:** This fulfills the communication failure set up in **Play Crack the Sky** (*"Flooded the engines and radio"*). He bragged about his silver tongue in *Deja*, realized he couldn't speak in **Degausser** (*"Never say anything right"*), and here, the phone line goes completely dead. 

#### 4. The Fire / The Loss of the "Bright"

*   **The Lyric:** *"I have burned the bush that covered my light / Even though I'm scared I won't burn that bright"*
*   **The Connection:** He references Moses's burning bush, but admits he is terrified his own soul/fire is too weak.
*   **The Evolution:** He is mourning the loss of the purity he mentioned in **Millstone** (*"I used to be such a burning example"*). This confirms the fear he expressed in **Jesus Christ**: *"'Cause my bright is too slight to hold back all my dark."* He eventually burns up completely in **Lit Me Up** (*"I burned from the inside out"*).

#### 5. The Execution / The Hangman

*   **The Lyric:** *"Hey hey hey, Mr. Hangman / Go get your rope"*
*   **The Connection:** He actively calls for his own execution to punish himself for his sins.
*   **The Evolution:** In **Seventy Times 7** (*YFW*), he told his enemy to *"Search your shelf for something which to hang yourself."* Now, he is the one calling for the rope. It joins the **Millstone** (*"Stone around my neck"*) as a method of self-inflicted execution.

#### 6. The Slippery Slope & The Daughters

*   **The Lyric:** *"Your daughters weren't careful / I fear that I am a slippery slope"*
*   **The Connection:** He views himself as a toxic, corrupting force to young women.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct callback to the predatory behavior of **Me vs. Maradona vs. Elvis**. It also sets up a massive meta-reference on the final album. In **In The Water** (*Sci-Fi*), he quotes this exact sentiment back to himself: *"Hide your daughters, the old men say / You were young once before, you know how we get our way."* 

#### 7. The Silver Plate / Decapitation

*   **The Lyric:** *"My smiling face that's on my head is on a silver plate"*
*   **The Connection:** Referencing the biblical execution of John the Baptist (head on a platter). 
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the anatomical deconstruction motif. He is offering up his own severed head. It connects to the command in **Degausser** (*"Take apart your head"*) and predicts the drug imagery in **At the Bottom** (*"I'd serve you drugs on a silver plate"*).

# Tracks 7 & 8: "Welcome to Bangkok" & "Not The Sun" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Welcome to Bangkok" serves as an instrumental palate cleanser built around a single, repeating phrase, acting as a bridge into "Not The Sun." The latter track is a frantic, driving song about rejecting an unfulfilling, superficial relationship. Together, these tracks reinforce Jesse Lacey's growing obsession with isolation, mechanical/industrial imagery, and the persistent fear of the ocean. They perfectly highlight the difference between real connection (family) and the empty gestures he has come to resent.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Space Cadet / Isolation in the Void ("Welcome to Bangkok")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Space cadet, pull out / Space cadet, pull out"*
*   **The Connection:** The repeated chant utilizes space exploration as a metaphor for being entirely disconnected from reality, drifting in a dangerous void, and needing to abort the mission.
*   **The Evolution:** Space is the ultimate vacuum of isolation in the Brand New universe. This directly connects to the album art of *Deja Entendu* (the lonely astronaut floating in space) and the self-insult in **Luca**: *"I am drearily bloodletting this bed wetting cosmonaut."* 

#### 2. The Machine / Mechanical Love ("Not The Sun")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Please don't be technology / So I can turn up your love like some cold machine"*
*   **The Connection:** He begs a partner not to be mechanical, while acknowledging that he wants to control their affection like a dial on a machine.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the "Machine" motif. In **Guernica**, he compared the failing human body to a toy with dead batteries. In **Jesus Christ**, the "machine" is the unfeeling, industrial nature of human cruelty/religion (*"We turn out hate in factories... we sleep inside of this machine"*). 

#### 3. The Waves & Suffocation ("Not The Sun")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Don't be waves, come to seal my fate marine... Don't be that hand around my throat so I can't breathe"*
*   **The Connection:** The relationship is framed as an aquatic execution (waves sealing a marine fate) and literal suffocation (hands around the throat). 
*   **The Evolution:** The nautical doom is inescapable. This links the shipwreck of **Play Crack the Sky**, the drowning weight of **Millstone**, and the concrete execution in **Luca** (*"There'll still be no air to breathe"*). The hands around the throat also mimic the lack of oxygen experienced underwater.

#### 4. The Bed (The Dead Space) ("Not The Sun")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Don't feed me scraps from your bed / I won't be the stray coming back just to be fed"*
*   **The Connection:** The bed is a site of pathetic, transactional intimacy. He refuses to accept the meager affection offered there.
*   **The Evolution:** The bed continues to be a place of misery rather than rest. It connects to the loneliness of **Jesus Christ** (*"Nobody in your bed"*) and the guilt of **Millstone** (*"Made this bed and I can't fall asleep in it"*). 

#### 5. Social Collapse: Friend vs. Family ("Not The Sun")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Say you're my friend, but why won't you be my family?"*
*   **The Connection:** He draws a hard line between casual relationships (friends) and permanent, unbreakable bonds (family), expressing frustration at the lack of the latter.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct response to his own behavior in the album opener. In **Sowing Season**, *he* was the one pushing people away: *"I'm not your friend / I'm not your family."* He caused the social collapse in **Millstone** (*"I was the glue that kept my friends together / Now they don't talk"*). By *Science Fiction*, he finally achieves the family bond he begs for here in **Could Never Be Heaven**: *"My wife's heart my own heart."*

#### 6. The Roots vs. The Fire ("Not The Sun")

*   **The Lyric:** *"How do I know where I am? (Burn it down) / I'm outside, I'm growing roots again"*
*   **The Connection:** He contrasts the destructive urge to burn everything with the slow, grounding process of growing roots.
*   **The Evolution:** This links the "Fire" motif with the "Earth/Grave" motif. The command to "burn it down" connects to **You Won't Know** (*"Burned the bush"*). The "roots" refer back to the burial imagery in **Sowing Season** (*"Get the seeds into the cold ground"*), which fully blooms in **Daisy** (*"Pray that they turn to seeds, to roots and then grass"*).

# Track 9: "Luca" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Luca" is a masterpiece of tension and one of the most vividly cinematic songs Brand New ever wrote. The title is a direct reference to Luca Brasi from *The Godfather*, famously known for the quote: *"Luca Brasi sleeps with the fishes."* This song is the absolute anchor point for all of the drowning/sinking metaphors in their discography. It marks the moment the "sinking" evolves from a tragic accident (a shipwreck) into an intentional execution (a mob hit). It is quiet, terrifying, and deeply rooted in the band's core imagery.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Concrete Shoes & The Lake (The Execution)

*   **The Lyric:** *"So we fixed you with cement galoshes / And no one can save you now / Unless you have friends among fish... still swimming alone in the lake"*
*   **The Connection:** The mob execution. He is drowning in a lake with concrete shoes.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the most brilliant multi-album continuity in their catalog. In "Luca," he is drowning in the lake. Three years later on *Daisy*, he returns to this exact location in **At the Bottom**: *"And there's a lake / And at the bottom you'll find all my friends / They don't swim 'cause they're all dead."* The friends among the fish didn't save him; they are dead down there with him. Finally, in **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*), he finds peace with it: *"At the bottom of the ocean / Fish won't judge you by your faults."*

#### 2. The Dialogue with "Son"

*   **The Lyric:** *"Son, the last thing you'll realize you need is what you've already got"*
*   **The Connection:** An older, wiser, or perhaps more cynical figure addressing a younger man as "son," offering a grim lesson about ungratefulness.
*   **The Evolution:** Jesse uses this specific conversational framing to close out his career. 11 years later, in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), Jesse *becomes* that older voice, speaking to his younger self (or his fans). But instead of a cynical lesson, he offers comfort: *"Don't lose hope, my son, this is the last one."*

#### 3. Dropping a Line (Communication and Salvation)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Please drop me a line with a hook and some raw bleeding bait / For I am uncaught"*
*   **The Connection:** A brilliant double-entendre. "Drop me a line" means both "call me/talk to me" and literally "drop a fishing line into the water to catch me." He wants to be rescued from the water, but no one is dropping the line.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the total failure of communication. In **Play Crack the Sky**, the radio flooded. In **You Won't Know**, he couldn't use the telephone. Here, no one drops the fishing line/phone line. By **Waste**, he is calling out to heaven, but *"we get no reply."*

#### 4. Space / The Cosmonaut

*   **The Lyric:** *"I am drearily bloodletting this bed wetting cosmonaut"*
*   **The Connection:** Space is the ultimate vacuum of isolation. He calls himself a "cosmonaut," but he is pathetic ("bed wetting") and self-harming ("bloodletting").
*   **The Evolution:** This connects to the album art of *Deja Entendu* (the lonely astronaut floating in space) and directly follows the chant of **Welcome to Bangkok** (*"Space cadet, pull out"*). The "bloodletting" also connects back to the medical/surgical motif of **Guernica** and **The Quiet Things**.

#### 5. Social Rejection & Inadequacy

*   **The Lyric:** *"You never worked well with our group / Not with the faults we found... We'll still find someone to be everything we know that you'll never be"*
*   **The Connection:** The execution in "Luca" isn't random; it is a punishment for failing to fit in or meet expectations. He is being replaced.
*   **The Evolution:** This directly echoes the social collapse in **Millstone** (*"I was the glue that kept my friends together / Now they don't talk"*) and the academic/spiritual failure in **Degausser** (*"Life is a test and I get bad marks"*). 

#### 6. The Disappearing Act

*   **The Lyric:** *"When I disappear, do you fear for the sister I took?"*
*   **The Connection:** He threatens to vanish entirely, taking someone with him. 
*   **The Evolution:** The desire to disappear is a recurring threat/coping mechanism. It started in **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot** (*"Every picture you paint, I will paint myself out"*) and culminates in the burial of **At the Bottom** (*"And when I lower it down / I'll let you fade away"*).

# Tracks 10 & 11: "Untitled" & "Archers" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Untitled" is a brief, haunting interlude consisting of a single, repeating line that perfectly bridges the isolation of "Luca" with the frantic, furious energy of "Archers." "Archers" is one of the most aggressive, driving rock songs on the album, functioning as a biting critique of organized religion, hypocrisy, and the destructive nature of carrying grudges. Together, these tracks heavily reinforce the communication blackout, the Fire motif, and the absurdity of the "Bureaucracy of Heaven."

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Communication/Reach Blackout ("Untitled")

*   **The Lyric:** *"I can never love you, I can never reach you"*
*   **The Connection:** Repeated like a broken record, this is the ultimate confession of distance. He cannot emotionally love, and he cannot physically or communicatively "reach" the subject.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the absolute dead end of the Communication motif. He sent the SOS (**Play Crack the Sky**), he used the telephone (**You Won't Know**), he dropped a line (**Luca**)—but ultimately, the line is dead. He can *never reach you*. This isolation leads directly to the silence of God in **Waste**: *"We get no reply."*

#### 2. The Fire & The Torch ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Who do you carry the torch for, my young man? ... Do you carry it around just to burn things down? ... You are freezing over hell"*
*   **The Connection:** He challenges someone holding onto a "torch" (a grudge or a self-righteous cause), asking if they are just using it to destroy things. 
*   **The Evolution:** The Fire motif is heavily weaponized here. It connects to **You Won't Know** (*"Burned the bush that covered my light"*). The irony of "freezing over hell" points to the fact that his actions are so cold and destructive they disrupt even the afterlife, linking to the final condemnation of **Noro** (*"I'm on my way to hell"*).

#### 3. Throwing Away the Tongue ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Throwing away this filthy, silver tongue / You're a voice that never sings, is what I say / If you try run in a maze of your lies / It's too hard to save"*
*   **The Connection:** A massive rejection of his past self. He actively wants to discard his ability to manipulate people with words.
*   **The Evolution:** In *Deja Entendu*, he bragged: *"My tongue's the only muscle on my body that works harder than my heart"* (**Okay I Believe You...**). Here, he calls that exact tongue "filthy" and acknowledges that the "maze of lies" he built is impossible to escape. The silver tongue is dead.

#### 4. The God in the Backseat ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"So we strap ourselves up to this engine now / With a god who we found laying under the backseat"*
*   **The Connection:** Blending the Car Crash motif with the Religion motif. Instead of a majestic gold chariot (**Jesus Christ**), God is a pathetic figure rolling around under the backseat of a speeding car, useless while they drive towards destruction.
*   **The Evolution:** This perfectly captures the theological exhaustion of the album. God isn't answering prayers (**Millstone**), Jesus said "Never again" (**Limousine**), and now God is just junk in the back of the car. It reinforces the absolute lack of divine control in the vehicle, predicting the inevitable crash of **Handcuffs** and **Waste**. 

#### 5. The "Book" of Hypocrisy ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"You're beating with a book everyone / That book tells you to love... 'Cause the god I believe in never worked on a campaign trail"*
*   **The Connection:** A direct attack on religious hypocrisy—using the Bible as a weapon to attack people rather than to love them. 
*   **The Evolution:** This cynicism regarding the "rules" of religion traces back to **Seventy Times 7** (*"They say you need to pray if you want to go to heaven"*). It also sets up the narrative of the paranoid, fundamentalist father in **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I made it clear to him what fear of God means."*

#### 6. Order Your Daughters ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"Order your daughters to ignore me"*
*   **The Connection:** He views himself as a toxic, dangerous presence that parents should protect their children from.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct callback to **You Won't Know** (*"Your daughters weren't careful / I fear that I am a slippery slope"*). It all culminates in the meta-callback of **In The Water** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Hide your daughters, the old men say / You were young once before, you know how we get our way."*

#### 7. Dust and Embers ("Archers")

*   **The Lyric:** *"There is a ember in the heart of the kiln... Burning out my sins until there's nothing but dust"*
*   **The Connection:** The desire to be purified by fire until he is reduced to nothing.
*   **The Evolution:** The "dust" imagery sets up the burial motif of *Daisy*. In **At the Bottom**, he sings: *"And then we all turned to dirt / And dust."* He wants to be burned away.

# Track 12: "Handcuffs" (*The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Handcuffs" is the chilling, bleak closer to *The Devil and God Are Raging Inside Me*. Written by guitarist Vincent Accardi (rather than Jesse Lacey), the song perfectly encapsulates the album's themes of guilt, powerlessness, and the desire for mutual destruction. The narrator wishes he had the authority to arrest, punish, or destroy the people who have wronged him—or perhaps even himself. It features the ultimate, fatal conclusion to the Car Crash motif and the darkest extension of the Drowning motif.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash (The Intentional Wreck)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'd drive my car off of the bridge / If I knew that you weren't inside / Put the pedal to the floor, who could ask for more? / A fantastic way to kill some time"*
*   **The Connection:** The Car Crash motif reaches its absolute, suicidal extreme. It is no longer an accident or a curse on someone else; it is an intentional, self-inflicted execution.
*   **The Evolution:** 
    *   *YFW:* "Crash your car" (Weaponized insult).
    *   *Deja:* "Stalk the idle kind in a car" (Predatory).
    *   *TDAG (Limousine/Millstone):* The drunk driver / Pedestrian accident (Tragedy).
    *   *TDAG (Handcuffs):* Drive the car off the bridge (Suicide).
    *   *Daisy (Noro):* "Our car's stuck on the train crossing" (Total powerlessness).
    *   *Sci-Fi (Waste):* "Shoe-in for the crash of the day" (Acceptance).

#### 2. Drowning the Innocent (The Ultimate Cruelty)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'd drown all these crying babies / If I knew that their mothers wouldn't cry"*
*   **The Connection:** The darkest, most shocking extension of the Water/Drowning motif. The narrator is so consumed by misery that he fantasizes about passing the drowning onto innocent children.
*   **The Evolution:** Water is almost always used to describe his *own* doom (**Play Crack the Sky**, **Luca**, **Millstone**). Here, the trauma is projected outward. It connects to the "passing of blame" mentioned in **The Quiet Things** (*"Their parents let them shoulder all the blame"*). In *Sci-Fi*, he finally resolves to protect his children from this exact doom (**Could Never Be Heaven**: *"My son's hands my hands"*).

#### 3. The Backseat (The Trap)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'd throw you down in the back seat / As if you'd committed a terrible crime"*
*   **The Connection:** The car is used as a mobile prison cell or a place of assault/abduction.
*   **The Evolution:** The "backseat" is consistently a site of moral failing. In **Sudden Death in Carolina** (*YFW*), he spent car rides *"drinking in the backseat."* In **Archers**, God is found *"laying under the backseat"* (useless and ignored). In "Handcuffs," the backseat is where the punishment occurs.

#### 4. The Law vs. God (The Desire for Authority)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'd arrest you if I had handcuffs / I'd arrest you if I had the time... I'd wait for you outside the courtroom / And taunt you when all your appeals were declined"*
*   **The Connection:** The narrator desires the ultimate authority to judge and punish, stepping into the role of the law (or God).
*   **The Evolution:** This desire to judge others connects directly to the "Bureaucracy of Heaven" motif. Because God is out of *"forgiveness supplies"* (**You Won't Know**) and Jesus said *"Never again"* (**Limousine**), the narrator wants to take justice into his own hands. However, he is powerless ("if I had handcuffs / if I had the time").

#### 5. "It's Hard to Be the Better Man"

*   **The Lyric:** *"It's hard be the better man / When you forget you're trying / It's hard to be the better man / When you're still lying"*
*   **The Connection:** The moral exhaustion of trying to be a good person, paired with the acknowledgment that he is fundamentally deceptive.
*   **The Evolution:** This summarizes the entire emotional arc of the album. He *wants* to be better, but he keeps lying (**Jesus Christ**: *"I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique"*). He realizes that because he is a liar with a "filthy silver tongue" (**Archers**), he cannot truly be the "better man."

#### 6. The Breakdown of the Home

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'd break into a towns worth of houses / And rob whole family's blind / I'd do it to you like you do it to me"*
*   **The Connection:** The destruction of the domestic space, driven by a desire for petty revenge.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the corruption of the home/bedroom. He is violating the safety of the house. By the next album, the home itself is a prison or a tomb (**Bed**: *"Everything that I own starts to pile up / Like bones make the walls of a prison"*).

# Track 1: "Vices" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Vices" is the chaotic, terrifying introduction to *Daisy*. The album opens with a crackling, vintage hymn ("On Life's Highway") about walking with God, which is abruptly and violently shattered by screaming, distortion, and the announcement of a suicide. If *The Devil and God* was the process of falling apart, *Daisy* is the sound of hitting the ground. This track officially abandons the search for grace and replaces it with an urgent, desperate need for numbness and destruction.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Fatal Leap (The Fall is Complete)

*   **The Lyric:** *"She said goodbye to the ground / And jumped"*
*   **The Connection:** The album immediately opens with a literal suicide. The tension is broken by a fatal jump.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the horrifying realization of the intrusive thought from **Jaws Theme Swimming** (*Deja*): *"Take a leap from out the window 'cause it's way too far to go through the door."* The jump has happened. It also directly contrasts with the ending of the discography. Here, she says goodbye to the ground and jumps. In **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), he finally decides to *"climb on up out of your grave."* 

#### 2. The Loss of the "Good Creator"

*   **The Lyric:** *"Our good Creator (oh no) / The dog in the manger"*
*   **The Connection:** He references God, but immediately follows it with "oh no," signaling a complete loss of faith. He then compares the Creator to a "dog in the manger"—a metaphor for someone who selfishly prevents others from having something they themselves do not need (in this case, peace or grace).
*   **The Evolution:** This is the final nail in the coffin for the "Bureaucracy of Heaven" motif. God stopped listening in **Millstone**, Jesus said "never again" in **Limousine**, and here, the Creator is viewed as actively spiteful or useless. This explains the later lyrics on the album (**In a Jar**: *"Too many saviors on my cross again"*).

#### 3. The Need for Numbness & Erasure

*   **The Lyric:** *"We need vices... Where is my shrink and my mind eraser?"*
*   **The Connection:** Because grace is gone, he needs artificial ways to cope. He begs for therapy or complete mental erasure.
*   **The Evolution:** The "mind eraser" is a direct callback to the titular machine in **Degausser** (a device that erases tape). The desire for a "shrink" sets up the entire premise of the final album, *Science Fiction*, which literally opens with a tape from a *"400 hours of intensive, individual therapy"* session (**Lit Me Up**). The "vices" (drugs/alcohol) mentioned here become the exhausting cycle described in **Waste**: *"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down."*

#### 4. The Burning Forest (The Fire Consumes Everything)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And that forest burned (do you see smoke?)... We need vices at fifteen thousand degrees"*
*   **The Connection:** The fire motif expands from a personal flame to a massive, uncontrollable wildfire.
*   **The Evolution:** In **Millstone**, he was a *"burning example."* In **You Won't Know**, he *"burned the bush."* Now, the entire forest is on fire. This connects directly to the unreleased track **Noro** (*"Burn down the forest"*) and the literal flames of **You Stole** (*"The flames are burning me in my bed"*). 

#### 5. Rewind and Regret

*   **The Lyric:** *"Those days are dead (forgive me)... We need ten years on rewind so we can return"*
*   **The Connection:** He realizes that the "salad days" are gone, and he desperately wishes he could turn back the clock by a decade.
*   **The Evolution:** Ten years prior to *Daisy* (2009) was 1999—the era right before the band formed and the *Your Favorite Weapon* timeline began. He wants to go back to before all the trauma happened. He ultimately accepts that you cannot rewind time in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie."*

#### 6. The Funeral Gate

*   **The Lyric:** *"We need vices and wheels to the funeral gate"*
*   **The Connection:** The vehicle motif returns, but instead of a joyride or a crash, the car is just a hearse taking them to the graveyard.
*   **The Evolution:** This sets up the relentless burial/grave imagery of *Daisy*. The "funeral gate" leads directly into **At the Bottom** (*"I carry this box to the proper place"*) and the burial of the seeds in the title track **Daisy**.

"Bed" takes one of Brand New’s oldest motifs—the isolation and trauma of the bedroom—and turns it into a slow, suffocating dirge. In *Deja Entendu* and *The Devil and God*, the bed was a place of insomnia, predatory behavior, or desperate hiding. Here, the bed is a site of total surrender and physical confinement. The song merges the biological decay of the relationship with the feeling of being imprisoned by your own history.

#### 1. The Bed (The Ultimate Trap)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I don't ever want to go to bed... Laid her on the bed"*
*   **The Connection:** He dreads going to bed, yet the chorus obsessively repeats the act of laying someone (or being laid) down on it, like a body on a slab.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the culmination of the Bed motif. In **Sic Transit Gloria** (*Deja*), the victim was *"lying in bed, completely motionless."* In **Degausser** (*TDAG*), he screamed to *"take apart your bed."* Here, the fight is over. The bed is no longer a place to sleep; it is where you place the dead or defeated. This transitions perfectly into the next track, **You Stole**, where the bed literally catches fire.

#### 3. The Fire and the Ghost

*   **The Lyric:** *"Your hair is on fire / You snuff the blaze / Turn to vapour then you float away"*
*   **The Connection:** The fire consumes the person, leaving nothing but smoke or a ghost.
*   **The Evolution:** This directly sets up the imagery in **Gasoline** (two tracks later): *"It feels like I'm trying / To hold smoke / I swear it's like dying / To catch a ghost."* It also links back to the "burning out" motif of **Millstone** and **You Won't Know**.

#### 5. Anatomy (Eyes as Lungs)

*   **The Lyric:** *"My eyes are lungs, I'm a prophet and I speak in tongues / I know how you'll die"*
*   **The Connection:** A bizarre, surreal mixing of body parts. He is "breathing" through his vision, and his words are incomprehensible prophecies of doom.
*   **The Evolution:** The obsession with the lungs/breathing tracks back to **Play Crack the Sky** (*"Water in my lungs"*) and **Guernica** (*"My lungs are fresh and yours to keep"*). The claim that he "speaks in tongues" is the final corruption of his "silver tongue" from *Deja Entendu*. #### 6. The Ambush on the Road

*   **The Lyric:** *"Ambushed on the road, stole your gold"*
*   **The Connection:** The road is no longer a place for a car crash; it is a place where you are violently robbed of your value.
*   **The Evolution:** The highway/road continues to be a site of trauma. This "ambush" predicts the robbery/thief motif of **You Stole** (*"You stole from the cradles they were rocked in"*). 

#### 7. The Prison of Bones (The House)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Everything that I own starts to pile up / Like bones make the walls of a prison"*
*   **The Connection:** His own possessions and history (represented as skeletons/bones) have trapped him inside his house.
*   **The Evolution:** The domestic space is completely corrupted. This connects to **Handcuffs** (*"Break into a towns worth of houses"*) and anticipates the bizarre self-surgery he performs at home in **Same Logic/Teeth** (*"So you decide to make incisions / At your home while you're alone... broke some bones"*).

# Track 3: "At The Bottom" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"At the Bottom" is the absolute nadir of the Brand New universe. It is the destination that the band had been sinking toward since 2001. All the water/drowning metaphors from *Deja Entendu* and *The Devil and God* officially end here, at the bottom of the lake, where everyone is dead. To cope with this, Jesse turns to the Earth motif—digging a grave to bury his past. This song is the direct prequel to the healing that takes place on *Science Fiction*. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Lake and the Dead Friends (The Drowning is Complete)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And there's a lake / And at the bottom you'll find all my friends / They don't swim 'cause they're all dead"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate, horrifying conclusion to the water motif. 
*   **The Evolution:** 
    *   In **Jude Law** (*YFW*), he wished an ex would *"drown at the bottom of the sea."* 
    *   In **Play Crack the Sky** (*Deja*), the ship sank. 
    *   In **Millstone** (*TDAG*), he tied the stone around his neck. 
    *   In **Luca** (*TDAG*), the mob threw him in the lake (*"still swimming alone in the lake"*). 
    *   Here, in **At the Bottom**, the swimming is over. The drowning is complete. He finds his friends (the ones he lost in *Sowing Season*) down there with him. 
    *   This sets up **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he finally stops fighting the water: *"You could turn to water and we'd all float on."*

#### 2. The Grave and the Dirt (Digging the Hole)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I should've started some years ago digging that hole / Well, I carry this box to the proper place / And when I lower it down... turned to dirt and dust"*
*   **The Connection:** Because the water is full of the dead, he turns to the earth to bury his trauma. He is conducting his own funeral.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the burial imagery from **Sowing Season** (*"get the seeds into the cold ground"*). But most importantly, it establishes the literal "grave" that he must escape in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"To climb on up out of your grave / With the bits of you you managed to save."* "At the Bottom" is the sound of him digging that exact grave. 

#### 3. Self-Inflicted Sabotage (The Dam)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I stole bricks from the dam almost everyday / Now I'm drowning in the flood I made"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits that the disaster was entirely his own fault. He dismantled his own protection brick by brick.
*   **The Evolution:** This answers the question from **Sowing Season** (*"Somebody threw that brick, shattered all your plans"*). *He* was the somebody. He threw the bricks. The "flood" he made here connects directly to the apocalyptic rain he begs for in the title track **Daisy** (*"Increase the flood... start things over again"*).

#### 4. The Anatomy of Deconstruction (Pulling Apart Lies)

*   **The Lyric:** *"'Cause I make little lies and then I pull them apart"*
*   **The Connection:** He dissects his own deceptions, unable to maintain the facade.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the continuation of the manic commands in **Degausser** (*"Take apart your head"*). He is obsessed with taking things apart to see how they work or why they fail. This culminates in the self-surgery of **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*).

#### 5. The Roadkill (The Visceral Car Crash)

*   **The Lyric:** *"A deer that a hunter shot in the heart / Some dogs that got hit by cars / All came to spill their guts"*
*   **The Connection:** The car crash motif takes a feral, gruesome turn. The road is just a place of meaningless slaughter.
*   **The Evolution:** The romantic joyrides of **Soco Amaretto Lime** (*"Speeding cars"*) are long gone. The road here is covered in literal "spilled guts" (a callback to **I Will Play My Game...**: *"I am paid to spill my guts"*). This meaningless carnage on the road prepares him for the apathy of **Waste**: *"Laying in the road / Shoe-in for the crash of the day."*

#### 6. Drugs and Altered States

*   **The Lyric:** *"Well, I'd serve you drugs on a silver plate / If I thought it would help you get away"*
*   **The Connection:** Substance abuse is viewed as a twisted act of mercy—a way to "get away" from the pain. (The "silver plate" is a callback to the decapitation imagery in **You Won't Know**).
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the theme of toxic coping mechanisms (like the alcohol in *Millstone* and *Limousine*). By *Science Fiction*, looking back, that constant cycle is exposed as a waste of time in **Waste**: *"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down... this is the last one."*

#### 7. Demanding Answers from God

*   **The Lyric:** *"Well, explain myself to me on the other side / I'll want some answers when I die"*
*   **The Connection:** He is no longer begging God for help; he is angry and demanding an accounting.
*   **The Evolution:** The evolution of his relationship with God is fascinating:
    *   *Millstone:* Thinks God stopped listening.
    *   *Jesus Christ:* Begs God for a sign.
    *   *At the Bottom:* Gets angry and *demands* answers.
    *   *Waste (Sci-Fi):* Accepts that there is "no reply" and finds his own peace.

#### 8. The Bed and Loneliness

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm alone now in my bed... Some die looking for a hand to hold"*
*   **The Connection:** The bed continues to be a site of isolation. The fear of dying alone ("looking for a hand to hold") is a direct echo of **Jesus Christ** (*"I will die all alone / Nobody in your bed"*).
*   **The Evolution:** He finally defeats this exact fear in **Waste**: *"You are not alone."*

# Track 4: "Gasoline" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Gasoline" is a frantic, explosive track that perfectly captures the feeling of pouring fuel on a fire you were supposed to put out. It is a song about compounding your own mistakes, the cowardice of running away from the mess you made, and the realization that the spiritual "sickness" infecting you cannot be cured by traditional means. It heavily reinforces the Fire motif, the failure of Religion, and the powerlessness of the Train/Vehicle motif.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Fire / Self-Sabotage

*   **The Lyric:** *"So you tried to put the fire out / But you used gasoline / And when the congregation gathered around / You screaming, 'It wasn't me!'"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate act of making a bad situation worse, followed immediately by lying and denying responsibility to the "congregation" (the audience/church).
*   **The Evolution:** This is the continuation of the self-sabotage motif from the previous track, **At the Bottom** (*"I stole bricks from the dam... drowning in the flood I made"*). He knows he is the one causing the destruction, but he refuses to admit it. The "gasoline" imagery returns as the ultimate destructive force on *Science Fiction* in **Lit Me Up**: *"It lit me up like a rag soaked in gasoline."* He finally lets the fire consume him.

#### 2. The Sickness & The Failure of Holy Water

*   **The Connection:** He equates his moral or emotional decay to a physical sickness/plague. Crucially, he notes that religion ("holy water") did not cure the sickness; it actually drowned the vaccine. 
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the "Medical/Surgical" motif established in **Guernica** (*"I am too weak to be your cure"*). By *Science Fiction*, he looks back at his audience in **Can't Get It Out** and asks: *"Were you one of the cured kids?"* Furthermore, the idea that "holy water" actually drowns the cure is a brilliant merger of the Water/Drowning motif with the Bureaucracy of Heaven motif—religion is actively harming him, not saving him.

#### 3. The Ghost & The Smoke (Trying to Hold Nothing)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I swear it's like dying / To catch a ghost / It feels like I'm trying / To hold smoke"*
*   **The Connection:** The futile, exhausting effort of trying to grasp something that isn't tangible (like fixing a relationship, finding faith, or regaining his youth).
*   **The Evolution:** This directly links back to the previous track, **Bed** (*"Turn to vapour then you float away"*). The "ghost" imagery also connects to the unreleased track **Missing You** and his description of himself as a disconnected spirit in **Out of Mana** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I'm a ghost / I can't say I know that I'm even here."*

#### 4. The Gun / Cowardice

*   **The Lyric:** *"When the army had to hold the line / Well, you were nowhere near the front... Well, you were loading up your gun"*
*   **The Connection:** He accuses himself (or someone else) of cowardice—arming himself far away from the actual danger while others suffer.
*   **The Evolution:** The gun is frequently used as a symbol of fear or violent paranoia rather than bravery. This connects to **Sudden Death in Carolina** (*"Lips taste like a loaded gun"*) and the paranoid father in **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Last night I heard a voice that said, 'Don't give up your gun'."*

#### 5. Isolation / "The Only One"

*   **The Lyric:** *"You think that no one else is lonesome? / You think that you're the only one?"*
*   **The Connection:** A harsh reality check regarding his own isolation. He challenges the self-pity that has defined his loneliness.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a crucial pivot point away from the "Die Alone" prophecy of *Deja/TDAG*. He is beginning to realize that his loneliness isn't unique. This exact realization is what allows him to finally find solidarity in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), replacing "I am the only one" with the mantra: *"You are not alone, you are not alone."*

#### 6. The Train (The Fatalistic Vehicle)

*   **The Lyric:** *"It feels like I'm jumping towards a train"*
*   **The Connection:** The Car Crash motif reaches its most fatalistic point. Cars can be steered or braked, but trains cannot. You are entirely powerless against them.
*   **The Evolution:** The vehicle motif on *Daisy* abandons steering wheels entirely. In **Sink**, he commands: *"And you go lay down on the track."* In **Bought a Bride**, the victim is *"laid on the train tracks."* Finally, in **Noro**, the trap is sprung: *"And our car's stuck / On the train crossing."* He has thrown himself in front of an unstoppable force.

# Track 5: "You Stole" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"You Stole" is a slow, methodical track that simmers with resentment and apathy. It is arguably the most exhausted-sounding song in the Brand New catalog. It deals heavily with the feeling of having your innocence or potential stolen from you, and the creeping realization that you don't even have the energy to fight back anymore. This song features the ultimate corruption of the "Bed" motif and the total breakdown of the "Car" motif.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Bed on Fire (Total Apathy)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Last night they said the fire had spread... And now the flames are burning me in my bed / But I just don't care"*
*   **The Connection:** The fire from "Vices" and "Gasoline" has reached his most intimate, isolated space. But instead of panicking, he just lies there and lets it burn him.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the absolute rock bottom of the Bed motif. 
    *   *YFW:* Hiding in bed (*Seventy Times 7*).
    *   *Deja:* Sneaking into beds to hurt people (*The Boy Who Blocked...*).
    *   *TDAG:* The insomnia of guilt (*Millstone*, *Degausser*).
    *   *Daisy:* The bed is literally on fire, and he is too depressed/apathetic to move. This total energetic depletion is finally resolved in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), where he admits that *"every night had you laid low,"* but tells himself, *"It's going to feel so good to let it go."*

#### 2. The Broken Down Cars (The Dead Engine)

*   **The Lyric:** *"We'll just sit around like broke down cars in the lot / Waiting for repairs"*
*   **The Connection:** The Vehicle motif loses all momentum. They aren't crashing, they aren't speeding—they are just broken and abandoned in a parking lot.
*   **The Evolution:** This perfectly mirrors his own physical and emotional breakdown. In **Soco Amaretto Lime** (*YFW*), he bragged: *"I got another 500 miles / Before we shut this engine down."* The 500 miles are up. The engine is dead. He is waiting for a "repair" (or a "cure," referencing the medical motif), which he ultimately has to attempt himself in **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*).

#### 3. Rust and Aging (The Loss of Youth)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And I wish that my condition was new, but I'm old and rusted"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates aging with the physical oxidation of metal—he is decaying. 
*   **The Evolution:** This references the manic breakdown of **Degausser** (*"I've been on pause, I'm shaking off the rust"*). It also acts as the grim reality check to the *"stay 18 forever"* promise of **Soco**. He is no longer new; he is old and rusted.

#### 4. The Communication Blackout (Nothing to Say)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And I'll pretend like I've got something to say / But I've got nothing"*
*   **The Connection:** The complete exhaustion of his ability to communicate. He has run out of words.
*   **The Evolution:** He used to brag about his words (**Okay I Believe You...**), then he realized his words were toxic (**Degausser**: *"Never say anything right"*), and now he admits he has absolutely nothing left to say. This connects directly to the meta-confession in **In The Water** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I can't say it enough / Can't sing it enough / So I'll find another way."*

#### 5. Stolen Youth / The Thief

*   **The Lyric:** *"From the cradles they were rocked in / You took the first words that they spoke / Yeah, you stole / So if I'm a liar and you're a thief..."*
*   **The Connection:** He accuses the subject of stealing innocence and potential right from the cradle. 
*   **The Evolution:** The "liar and thief" imagery connects back to **Jesus Christ** (*"I know you're coming in the night like a thief / But I've had some time alone to hone my lying technique"*). Furthermore, the anxiety over "the cradle" and the theft of childhood innocence explains why he is so fiercely protective of his own children in **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I'd hold him down on the Tompkins Lake / Before I ever let him go to the wolves."* He refuses to let his children be stolen.

#### 6. Knowing Where the Other Sleeps (The Final Threat)

*   **The Lyric:** *"At least we both know where the other one sleeps / So let's end this tonight"*
*   **The Connection:** A mutually assured destruction. Because they both know each other's most vulnerable space (the bed/where they sleep), they can destroy each other.
*   **The Evolution:** This links the vulnerability of sleep back to **Guernica** (*"I will watch you while you're sleeping"*) and the predatory threat of **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot** (*"When I sneak to your bed to pour salt in your wounds"*). Sleep is never safe.

# Track 6: "Be Gone" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Be Gone" is the strangest, most jarring track on *Daisy*. Acting as a short, glitchy interlude, Jesse's vocals are heavily manipulated, stuttering and pitch-shifted to the point of sounding synthetic and broken. Lyrically, it is a brief burst of paranoia, focusing on the inability to stay awake, the fear of divine judgment, and the terrifying lack of control inside a speeding vehicle. It perfectly bridges the apathy of "You Stole" with the violent surrender of "Sink."

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Glitch / The Broken Machine

*   **The Musical Delivery:** The vocals are chopped, distorted, and stuttered.
*   **The Connection:** The actual sound of the song represents the "Machine" motif. The singer is malfunctioning.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the sonic realization of the mechanical breakdowns mentioned in **Guernica** (*"Is this the way a toy feels when its batteries run dry?"*) and **Not The Sun** (*"Turn up your love like some cold machine"*). He has literally been "degaussed" (wiped/scrambled). It also predicts the digital hellscape described in **Out of Mana** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Hell is digital / You've smashed to smithereens."*

#### 2. The Insomnia / Fear of Sleep

*   **The Lyric:** *"It's getting hard to stay up"*
*   **The Connection:** Following the burning bed in **You Stole**, he is fighting sleep, likely because he is terrified of what happens when he loses consciousness.
*   **The Evolution:** The battle against sleep stretches back to **Degausser** (*"Goodbye to sleep / I think this staying up is exactly what I need"*). By the end of this album, this exact fear infects everyone in **Noro**: *"Why won't anyone just close their eyes? / Could it hurt them to rest for a while?"*

#### 3. God Calling / The Heartbreak

*   **The Lyric:** *"I think I can hear God calling my name / He'll try to make my heart break"*
*   **The Connection:** He hears a divine voice, but assumes its only purpose is to cause him pain. 
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the cynical, adversarial relationship with God on *Daisy*. In **Millstone**, he thought God wasn't listening. Here, God is calling him, but Jesse views it as an attack. This paranoia regarding the voice of God peaks in **Desert** (*Sci-Fi*), where the narrator is driven to violence by what he hears: *"Last night I heard a voice that said, 'Don't give up your gun'."*

#### 4. The Runaway Car (Total Powerlessness)

*   **The Lyric:** *"They won't slow the car down / I'm scared that I will keep it for my own"*
*   **The Connection:** He is trapped in a moving vehicle, and whoever is driving refuses to hit the brakes. He is completely out of control.
*   **The Evolution:** The Car Crash motif reaches maximum anxiety. 
    *   In **Millstone**, the driver caused the tragedy (*"They never hit their brakes"*).
    *   In **Handcuffs**, he wanted to drive the car off the bridge himself.
    *   Here, he is trapped in the passenger seat (or the backseat, tying back to **Sudden Death in Carolina** and **Archers**), screaming for the car to slow down. This lack of control leads directly to the fatalistic collision in **Noro**: *"Our car's stuck / On the train crossing."*

#### 5. "Come Down"

*   **The Lyric:** *"The one that is calling my name / Is telling me come down"*
*   **The Connection:** The voice is urging him to descend, either physically (from a ledge/high/pride) or spiritually (into hell).
*   **The Evolution:** This phrase features heavily in the band's lexicon. In **Vices**, the girl jumped down. In **Okay I Believe You...**, he mocked people: *"Hope you come down with something they can't diagnose."* Most importantly, "coming down" becomes the defining phrase for his substance abuse cycle in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Every night you were tripping out / In the morning you were coming down."*

# Track 7: "Sink" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Sink" is the turning point of the entire *Daisy* album. After years of fighting the ocean, struggling with concrete shoes, and panicking about the ship going down, Jesse Lacey finally throws his hands up and surrenders. He stops trying to save people (or himself) from the water and the tracks. It is a violent, aggressive permission slip to self-destruct. This track explicitly ties together the Water, Fire, Earth, and Vehicle motifs into one massive declaration of apathy.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Surrender to the Water (The Title Motif)

*   **The Lyric:** *"If you call then I'm coming to get you / You want to sink, so I'm gonna let you"*
*   **The Connection:** After years of begging for lifelines (**Millstone**: *"Throw me that lifeline"*) and dropping lines to catch people (**Luca**: *"Drop me a line with a hook"*), he finally gives up. If you want to drown, he will let you.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the resolution to the panic of *Deja* and *TDAG*. He accepts the aquatic doom introduced in **Jude Law** (*"Drowning at the bottom of the sea"*) and **Tautou** (*"I'm sinkin' like a stone"*). This total surrender paves the way for the healing on *Science Fiction*. Because he stops fighting the sinking here, he eventually learns how to float in **Waste**: *"You could turn to water and we'd all float on."*

#### 2. The Train Track Execution (The Vehicle Motif)

*   **The Lyric:** *"But first I must find my way back / And you go lay down on the track"*
*   **The Connection:** Following the runaway car in **Be Gone**, the vehicle motif becomes entirely fatalistic. He instructs the subject to just lay down in front of a train.
*   **The Evolution:** The loss of the steering wheel is complete. This connects to **Gasoline** (*"Jumping towards a train"*) and **Bought a Bride** (*"Laid her on the train tracks"*). It all culminates in the final trap of **Noro**: *"Our car's stuck on the train crossing."*

#### 3. The Bedroom on Fire (Again)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Then the fire snuck into your bedroom / Now I'm falling asleep to forget you"*
*   **The Connection:** The fire returns to the most intimate space. Instead of panicking or trying to put it out, his response is to just go to sleep.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct sequel to the apathy of **You Stole** (*"And now the flames are burning me in my bed / But I just don't care"*). The use of sleep as an escape mechanism contrasts sharply with the insomnia of **Degausser** (*"Goodbye to sleep"*). He is so exhausted he can sleep through a literal fire.

#### 4. The Nautical Doom (The Moat & The Oars)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Turn the cannons towards the boat / Men were drowning in the moat / It was the end of all the rowers oars"*
*   **The Connection:** Returning to the nautical warfare imagery of **Play Crack the Sky**. The boat is destroyed, and the men are drowning.
*   **The Evolution:** The "rowers oars" is a brilliant callback to the tragedy of **Limousine**, where he described the dying girl as a *"tiny boat, with oars / Feather oars."* Here, the oars are broken, meaning all forward momentum and control are officially lost. 

#### 5. Earth and Worms (The Burial)

*   **The Lyric:** *"All I want's some earth and seed... It's taken me this long to learn / That every dead is ate by worms / And once they're gone they don't return"*
*   **The Connection:** He accepts the finality of death and burial.
*   **The Evolution:** The "earth and seed" is a direct repetition of the burial motif from **Sowing Season** (*"Get the seeds into the cold ground"*) and **Daisy** (*"Pray that they turn to seeds, to roots"*). The grim realization that the "dead is ate by worms" connects to **At the Bottom** (*"Turned to dirt and dust"*). It is the complete rejection of resurrection. 

#### 6. The Rope / The Mark

*   **The Lyric:** *"I don't want to let you go / But it hurts my hands to hold the rope / I won't be such an easy mark"*
*   **The Connection:** Letting go of the rope (either a lifeline or a noose) because it is causing him pain. 
*   **The Evolution:** This connects to the hanging motif. In **You Won't Know**, he called for the Hangman to *"go get your rope."* In the unreleased **Battalions**, he notes: *"I know you want to untie this rope and let it go."* Here, he finally lets it go.

#### 7. Satan in Long Words (The Death of the Silver Tongue)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I see God in birds and Satan in long words"*
*   **The Connection:** He equates complex, manipulative language with the devil.
*   **The Evolution:** The absolute destruction of the arrogant persona from *Deja Entendu*. He used to brag about his vocabulary (**Okay I Believe You**: *"These are the words you wish you wrote down"* / **The Quiet Things**: *"Wasting words on lowercases and capitals"*). Now, he views his own "long words" as evil. He realizes his "silver tongue" (**Archers**) is satanic.

# Track 8: "Bought a Bride" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Bought a Bride" is a jagged, cynical critique of modern suburban life, marriage, and the commodification of human connection. It portrays people not as individuals, but as biological components of a larger, unfeeling machine (the city). It heavily features themes of emotional exhaustion, the failure of domestic life, and the violent intersection of human bodies and vehicle infrastructure (the train tracks). It also includes one of Jesse's most profound meta-confessions regarding his own songwriting.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. Anatomy of the City (Blood Cells & Oxygen)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Cars are little blood cells / We are oxygen / City is the airways / Suburbs appendages"*
*   **The Connection:** He zooms out to view the entire highway and city infrastructure as a giant biological system. Cars are just cells pumping through veins. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the macro-level version of the "Machine" motif. In **Jesus Christ**, humans were cogs (*"We sleep inside of this machine"*). In **Guernica**, he wanted to shrink down and *"sink through your skin to your blood cells."* Here, he equates the sprawling American suburbs to a giant, unfeeling body. It highlights how small and insignificant human life is within the system.

#### 2. The Empty House (The Dead Domestic Space)

*   **The Lyric:** *"If everyone's a structure / Where their own savior sits / Then I'm a little red house / But no one's living in it"*
*   **The Connection:** Using a house as a metaphor for his soul, he admits he is completely empty inside.
*   **The Evolution:** The domestic space is almost always cursed in the Brand New universe. It connects to the *"empty hotel"* in **The Quiet Things**, the *"dead bedroom"* in **Magazines**, and the house that becomes a *"prison"* of bones in **Bed**. Ultimately, this imagery returns in *Science Fiction* during the therapy dream of **Lit Me Up**: *"It's where you live, but you don't know how it's built."*

#### 3. The Train Track Execution (The Recurring Nightmare)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Little cities names labeled along the map / They tied her up and laid her on the train tracks"*
*   **The Connection:** The classic, old-west trope of tying a victim to the train tracks is used to describe the suffocating trap of modern domestic life (or perhaps a literal murder). 
*   **The Evolution:** This solidifies the "Train" as the ultimate vehicle of doom on *Daisy*. He warned about *"jumping towards a train"* in **Gasoline**, he told someone to *"lay down on the track"* in **Sink**, and here, the execution is carried out. This total lack of control ends the album in **Noro**: *"Our car's stuck on the train crossing."*

#### 4. The Meta-Confession (Never Sing Again)

*   **The Lyric:** *"If somehow I was new and everything was unsaid / I'd go and buy a hammer, never sing again"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits that his entire career as a singer/songwriter is a mistake born of trauma. If he could erase his past ("unsaid"), he would gladly do manual labor (buy a hammer) and quit music forever.
*   **The Evolution:** The culmination of his guilt over his own art. He complained about songwriting in **Failure By Design**, admitted he lied in his songs in **Good to Know...**, and felt guilty about selling sadness in **I Will Play My Game...**. This exact sentiment—wishing he could just do manual labor instead of singing—returns explicitly in **Lit Me Up** (*Sci-Fi*): *"I want to put my hands to work until the work's done."*

#### 5. Trees, Wood, and Prosthetics

*   **The Lyric:** *"Trees will make a forest / Trees will make a bow... Down in Jersey lumber / Still in prosthetic limbs"*
*   **The Connection:** He focuses on wood and trees being repurposed for violent or artificial means (bows, prosthetics, lumber).
*   **The Evolution:** Wood is consistently tied to violence and crucifixion. This traces directly back to **Jesus Christ**: *"We all got wood and nails / We turn out hate in factories."* The idea of "prosthetic limbs" also connects to the unreleased **1996**: *"With the weight of the world on a prosthetic shoulder."*

#### 6. The Apathetic Soldier

*   **The Lyric:** *"Should've been a soldier / I could've fought and died / There's no revolution / So I bought a bride"*
*   **The Connection:** He wishes for a noble death or a grand cause, but finding none in the modern world, he settles for the transactional, hollow life of "buying a bride."
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the military/war imagery from the unreleased track **Brother's Song** (*"What sending a kid to fight can really do"*). He realizes there is no glory (*"Glory Fades"*), only empty transactions.

# Track 9: "Daisy" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
The title track "Daisy" serves as the ideological thesis for the album. Bookended by samples of a vintage religious broadcast, the song deals heavily with the desire for a total, apocalyptic reset. Jesse lists all the things he is supposed to be—a mountain, a river, a sun—and systematically details how he has failed at all of them. The song culminates in a desperate plea for either a mass burial (earth) or a biblical flood (water) to wipe the slate clean.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Sermon Sample (The Direct *Sci-Fi* Callback)

*   **The Lyric:** *"And we sing this morning that wonderful and grand old message / Well, I don't know about you, but I never get tired about it / Number 99: Just As I Am"*
*   **The Connection:** An audio sample of a mid-century preacher introducing a hymn.
*   **The Evolution:** This specific audio sample is arguably the most famous meta-reference in the band's history. 8 years later, at the very end of the song **In The Water** (*Sci-Fi*), Brand New replays this exact same audio clip. By doing so, they explicitly link the trauma of *Daisy* with the therapy/healing of *Science Fiction*, proving that the two albums are in direct conversation with each other. 

#### 2. The Failed Metaphors (The Dead Earth & Water)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm a mountain that has been moved / I'm a river that is all dried up / I'm an ocean nothing floats on"*
*   **The Connection:** He uses massive geographical features to describe his own impotence.
*   **The Evolution:** The line *"I'm an ocean nothing floats on"* is the absolute rock-bottom of the Water motif. In **Play Crack the Sky**, he drowned in it. In **At the Bottom**, his friends were dead in it. Here, the ocean is completely dead—it cannot even support buoyancy. This total failure makes the resolution in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*) so powerful: *"Turn to water and we'd all float on."* He finally makes the ocean work again.

#### 3. The Failed Metaphors (The Dead Fire & Communication)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm a sun that doesn't burn hot / I'm a moon that never shows its face / I'm a mouth that doesn't smile / I'm a word that no one ever wants to say"*
*   **The Connection:** He systematically denies his own heat, light, and linguistic value.
*   **The Evolution:** 
    *   *The Sun/Fire:* He lost his "burning example" in **Millstone**, burned the bush in **You Won't Know**, and here, the sun is completely cold. 
    *   *The Word/Mouth:* The final death of the "silver tongue." In **Degausser**, he *"never says anything right."* Here, he is a word that is universally hated.

#### 4. The Preacher Without a Pulpit (Religious Burnout)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm a preacher with no pulpit / Spewing a sermon that goes on and on"*
*   **The Connection:** He views himself as a useless religious figure, rambling without a platform or a point.
*   **The Evolution:** This references his earlier Messiah complex (**Okay I Believe You...**: *"I am heaven sent"*). By *Sci-Fi*, he looks back at this exact feeling in **Can't Get It Out**: *"Got my Messiah impression / I think I got it nailed down... I've got a positive message / Sometimes I can't get it out."* 

#### 5. The Mass Burial (Seeds & Grass)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Well if we take all these things and we bury them fast / And we pray that they turn to seeds, to roots and then grass / It'd be all right, it's all right"*
*   **The Connection:** The desire to bury his trauma and failures, hoping they will fertilize something new.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct continuation of **Sowing Season** (*"Time to get the seeds into the cold ground"*). He spent the entirety of **At the Bottom** *"digging that hole... turned to dirt and dust."* He wants the earth to swallow his mistakes. 

#### 6. The Biblical Flood (The Reset)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Or if the sky opened up and started pouring rain / Like he knew it was time to start things over again / It'd be all right... (Increase the flood)"*
*   **The Connection:** If burial doesn't work, he begs God for a global, apocalyptic flood to wash the earth clean and start over. 
*   **The Evolution:** The flood imagery traces back to **At the Bottom** (*"Drowning in the flood I made"*) and **Jude Law** (*"Drink for 40 days and 40 nights"*). But most importantly, the desire to "start things over again" is the exact origin of the mantra in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"You get to reset."* The flood he begs for in 2009 is the emotional reset he finally achieves in 2017.

# Track 10: "In a Jar" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"In a Jar" is a claustrophobic, desperate track that focuses heavily on the feeling of being trapped, suffocated, and surrounded by false prophets. The title suggests being caught and put on display, much like the "fireflies" mentioned in the lyrics. This song officially fractures the "Millstone" into smaller pieces of crushing weight, explicitly calls out the failure of saviors, and fully embraces the Water/Drowning motif as an inescapable reality. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Fractured Millstone (Stones in the Anatomy)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Stones in your eyes / Stones in your mouth / Stones in your ears / Stones in your mind"*
*   **The Connection:** The weight that he carries is no longer just around his neck; it has invaded his sensory organs and his brain. He is being blinded, silenced, and deafened by the weight.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the horrifying expansion of **Millstone** (*TDAG*): *"I'm my own stone around my neck."* Because he didn't deal with the stone, it broke apart and infected his entire anatomy. This sensory deprivation also links to the physical decay mentioned in **Same Logic/Teeth** (*Sci-Fi*): *"Cracked your head and broke some bones."* 

#### 2. The Claustrophobia / The Jar

*   **The Lyric:** *"We live in a jar, think the lids the sky... Stars are just a million little fireflies / The sun is just a hole, what is the light outside?"*
*   **The Connection:** He realizes that his entire worldview is a tiny, manufactured prison. The light he sees isn't real; it's just holes poked in the lid so he can breathe.
*   **The Evolution:** This connects to the prison imagery of **Bed** (*"Like bones make the walls of a prison"*). The mention of "fireflies" is a direct, tragic callback to the explosive car crash in **Limousine**: *"You explode / You firefly."* 

#### 3. Too Many Saviors (The Bureaucracy of Heaven)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You're hoping for a savior on your cross outside... There's too many saviors on my cross again / I know I'm never gonna be a perfect man"*
*   **The Connection:** A deeply cynical view of martyrdom and religion. He feels crowded by people (or religious figures) claiming they can save him, but he knows they can't.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the absolute dead-end of his search for grace. In **Jesus Christ**, he begged the Savior for a sign. In **Limousine**, the "sad Messiah" said *"Never again."* Here, he actively rejects the saviors because there are too many of them, and none of them work. 

#### 4. Total Surrender to Sinking

*   **The Lyric:** *"No one save me cause I'm / Sinking, drowning, but I'm / Struggling to save my life"*
*   **The Connection:** The definitive statement of the Drowning motif on this album. He explicitly tells people *not* to save him, even though he is still instinctively struggling.
*   **The Evolution:** Following the command in **Sink** (*"You want to sink, so I'm gonna let you"*), he applies the rule to himself. He is drowning at the bottom of the lake (**Luca**, **At the Bottom**). He finally gives up the struggle entirely on *Science Fiction*, letting the water carry him in **Waste**: *"You could turn to water and we'd all float on."*

#### 5. Sleep and the Seventh Day

*   **The Lyric:** *"Holding out for rest but on the seventh day / I've created nothing and I'm wide awake"*
*   **The Connection:** A biblical reference to God resting on the seventh day after creating the world. Jesse, however, has created nothing of value, and his insomnia prevents him from resting.
*   **The Evolution:** The insomnia motif continues to rage. Connecting back to **Degausser** (*"Goodbye to sleep"*) and **Be Gone** (*"It's getting hard to stay up"*), sleep is a luxury he is denied. The "seventh day" imagery also pairs with the "forty days and forty nights" from **Jude Law**, cementing his use of biblical timeframes to measure his misery. 

#### 6. The End of the Song (Meta-Narrative)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I love you most in song, but now it has to end"*
*   **The Connection:** He breaks the fourth wall to admit that his romantic/emotional connections only really exist within the confines of his songwriting, and the song is over.
*   **The Evolution:** This directly sets up the ultimate finale in **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*): *"This is the last one."* He realizes his art is a poor substitute for real connection, leading to the confession in **In The Water**: *"I can't sing it enough / So I'll find another way."*

# Track 11: "Noro" (*Daisy*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Noro" is the apocalyptic, exhausted finale of *Daisy*. If the album began with a jump to the ground (*Vices*), it ends with Jesse trapped in a car on the train tracks, bracing for impact and accepting his descent into hell. The song acts as a horrifying synthesis of the Fire, Insomnia, Car Crash, and Religion motifs. It is the absolute darkest moment in the Brand New discography, leaving no hope for salvation—only the terrifying wait for the end. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Car Crash (The Ultimate Trap)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Getting dark / Running out of luck / And our car's stuck / On the train crossing"*
*   **The Connection:** The absolute climax of the Vehicle motif. He is trapped in a car on the tracks, waiting for the train. There is zero control, no steering wheel, and no escape.
*   **The Evolution:** 
    *   *YFW:* "Crash your car" (Weaponized).
    *   *TDAG:* The drunk driver / "He ran out in the street" (Tragedy).
    *   *TDAG:* "Drive my car off of the bridge" (Suicidal).
    *   *Daisy:* The runaway car in **Be Gone** / "Lay down on the track" in **Sink**. 
    *   *Here:* The fatalistic trap. He is stuck. This explains why, by **Waste** (*Sci-Fi*), he is *"Laying in the road / Shoe-in for the crash of the day."* He knows the crash is inevitable.

#### 2. The Final Condemnation (The Highway to Hell)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm on my way to hell / (Well, I've tried, God knows that I've tried) / I'm on my way to hell"*
*   **The Connection:** After years of questioning his fate, he accepts his damnation as a certainty. He tried to be good, but he failed.

# Track 1: "Lit Me Up" (*Science Fiction*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Lit Me Up" is the haunting, slow-burn opener to Brand New's final album. It immediately sets the tone for the entire record: this is not a rock album; it is a therapy session. The song opens with an actual audio tape from a psychological evaluation, explicitly framing the next hour of music as the process of dissecting 16 years of trauma. Lyrically, Jesse Lacey embraces the "Fire" motif, but instead of trying to put the fire out or run from it, he allows it to completely consume him so he can finally be purified. 

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Therapy Tape (The Meta-Framework)

*   **The Lyric:** *"This tape recounts a dream / Which occurred close to the termination of approximately 400 hours of intensive, individual therapy... I don't mind having all this going on inside of me / It's sort of, I think I'm going to be relieved when it's over"*
*   **The Connection:** The album officially opens by declaring that the patient (Jesse/the band) has undergone massive psychological repair, and the goal is *relief*. 
*   **The Evolution:** This directly answers the desperate plea in **Vices** (*Daisy*): *"Where is my shrink and my mind eraser?"* He finally found the shrink. The entire album functions as the "mind eraser" or the "degausser"—a way to finally lay the past to rest.

#### 2. The Burning (Purification by Fire)

*   **The Lyric:** *"It lit me up like a rag soaked in gasoline... It lit me up and I burned from the inside out / Yeah, I burned like a witch in a Puritan town"*
*   **The Connection:** The fire is no longer an external threat or a mistake; it is an internal, all-consuming event. He compares himself to a witch being burned at the stake (an execution/purification).
*   **The Evolution:** This is the ultimate climax of the Fire motif. 
    *   *YFW:* He "threw up fire" (**Sudden Death...**).
    *   *TDAG:* He was a "burning example" (**Millstone**) but he "burned the bush" (**You Won't Know**).
    *   *Daisy:* He threw "gasoline" on his mistakes, and the fire spread to his bed (**You Stole**). 
    *   Here, he stops fighting the flames. The rag soaked in gasoline finally ignites him. He accepts the execution.

#### 3. Opening the Heart Like the Ocean

*   **The Lyric:** *"I want to open up my heart like the ocean"*
*   **The Connection:** He desires vast, deep emotional vulnerability—the exact opposite of the hardened, cynical persona he wore for years.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a massive reversal of two major motifs:
    *   *The Ocean:* In **Daisy**, the ocean was dead: *"I'm an ocean nothing floats on."* Here, he wants to open it up, preparing for the healing of **Waste**: *"Turn to water and we'd all float on."*
    *   *The Heart:* In **The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot**, his heart was *"harder than stone."* He is finally breaking the stone and opening the heart. 

#### 4. The Anatomy of the Beast

*   **The Lyric:** *"Don't cut me up and tell me that it's okay... It's the beast, it's my heart, it's open / Dive down into its unit in its head"*
*   **The Connection:** The clinical, surgical language continues. He identifies his own heart/mind as a "beast" that needs to be explored.
*   **The Evolution:** The refusal to be "cut up" references the self-surgery motif that dominates the album (**Same Logic/Teeth**: *"You decide to make incisions"*). The command to "dive down into its head" directly links to the healing mandate in **Waste**: *"Take your head apart, free your own heart."* 

#### 5. The Empty House (The Uncharted Mind)

*   **The Lyric:** *"It's where you live, but you don't know how it's built"*
*   **The Connection:** He uses architectural imagery to describe his own brain. He inhabits his mind, but he doesn't understand the psychological structures that govern it.
*   **The Evolution:** This connects to the corrupted domestic spaces of previous albums (**Bed**: *"Bones make the walls of a prison"*; **Bought a Bride**: *"I'm a little red house, but no one's living in it"*). He is finally attempting to map the house. 

#### 6. The Working Man vs. The Singer

*   **The Lyric:** *"I want to put my hands to work until the work's done"*
*   **The Connection:** A desire for simple, honest, manual labor as an escape from the emotional exhaustion of his art.
*   **The Evolution:** This is a direct callback to the meta-confession in **Bought a Bride** (*Daisy*): *"I'd go and buy a hammer, never sing again."*

# Track 2: "Can't Get It Out" (*Science Fiction*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Can't Get It Out" sounds like an upbeat, 90s alternative rock anthem, but the lyrics are a cynical, exhausted examination of Jesse Lacey's own legacy. It is a song about the frustration of wanting to write a positive message but being bogged down by clinical depression and the heavy expectations of his fanbase. He directly addresses his past arrogance ("Messiah impression") and the "Car Crash" motif, acknowledging the damage done while trying to figure out how to move forward.

*   **The Lyric:** *"You had me caught in your headlights / You were running me down"*

*   **The Evolution:** This references the fatal pedestrian accident in **Millstone** (*TDAG*): *"He just ran out in the street."* However, the tone here is crucial: he is singing about it in the past tense, and he survived it. This prepares the listener for the exhausted acceptance of the crash in **Waste**: *"Shoe-in for the crash of the day."*

*   **The Lyric:** *"Thought I was a creator / I'm here just hanging around / Got my Messiah impression / I think I got it nailed down"*

*   **The Evolution:** This is the absolute dismantling of the arrogance from *Deja Entendu*. In **Okay I Believe You**, he claimed, *"I am heaven sent... This is the grace only we can bestow."* In **Limousine**, the real Messiah told him *"Never again."* Now, he admits his entire God-complex was just an "impression."

*   **The Evolution:** He has always struggled with the purpose of his songwriting. In **I Will Play My Game**, he hated that he was *"paid to make girls panic."* In **You Stole**, he admitted, *"I pretend like I've got something to say / But I've got nothing."* Here, he finally has something good to say, but he physically cannot get the words out. #### 4. The "Cured Kids" (The Medical Motif)

*   **The Connection:** His courage was hidden underground, beneath the domestic space.

*   **The Connection:** He physically aches for the fake ("replica") feeling of being young again, but ultimately wants to eject from the cycle entirely. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the final nail in the coffin for the *"stay 18 forever"* promise of **Soco Amaretto Lime**. He admits that trying to hold onto youth is painful and fake. This perfectly sets up the very next track, **Waste**, where he officially calls the "salad days" a "damn lie."

# Track 3: "Waste" (*Science Fiction*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Waste" is the emotional fulcrum of the entire Brand New discography. Placed early on their final album, it serves as a meta-conversation where the older, wiser Jesse Lacey speaks directly to his younger, self-destructive self—and by extension, the fans who grew up with him. It systematically addresses, resolves, and puts to rest almost every major motif the band ever constructed: the car crash, the drowning, the silence of God, and the fear of dying alone. It is the sound of exhausted, hard-won healing.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Resolution of "Soco Amaretto Lime" (The Salad Days)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You and I were stuck in the waste / Talking about our salad days / What a damn lie"*
*   **The Connection:** "Salad days" is an idiom for youth and innocence. Jesse looks back at his obsession with staying young and calls it a lie.
*   **The Evolution:** This officially destroys the promise made in 2001's **Soco Amaretto Lime** (*"I'm gonna stay 18 forever"*). He realizes that trying to hold onto the past was a "waste" of time.

#### 2. The Resolution of the Water Motif (Floating On)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Well, you could turn to water and we'd all float on"*
*   **The Connection:** Instead of fighting the ocean, he suggests becoming part of it and floating. (A thematic nod to Modest Mouse's song "Float On").
*   **The Evolution:** After 16 years of drowning, this is the cure. 
    *   *YFW:* "Drowning at the bottom" (**Jude Law**)
    *   *Deja:* "I'm sinkin' like a stone" (**Tautou**)
    *   *TDAG:* "Ship went down in sight of land" (**Jesus Christ**)
    *   *Daisy:* "I'm an ocean nothing floats on" (**Daisy**)
    *   *Here:* The water is no longer fatal. He has learned how to float. 

#### 3. The Resolution of the Anatomy Motif (Take Your Head Apart)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Take your head apart, free your own heart"*
*   **The Connection:** He commands himself to deconstruct his mind, not as a punishment, but to release the pain trapped inside.
*   **The Evolution:** The frantic, panicked command from **Degausser** (*TDAG*): *"Take apart your head"* is repeated here, but the tone is completely different. In 2006, it was a manic breakdown. In 2017, it is a therapeutic exercise. 

#### 4. The Resolution of the Bureaucracy of Heaven (No Reply)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Always begging for some grace / When there's no time, we get no reply"*
*   **The Connection:** He accepts that God (or the universe) is not going to answer him. He has to find his own grace.
*   **The Evolution:** This ends the theological panic of the band. Because God stopped listening in **Millstone**, Jesus said "Never again" in **Limousine**, and heaven was out of "forgiveness supplies" in **You Won't Know**, Jesse finally accepts the silence. 

#### 5. The Resolution of the Car Crash Motif (Laying in the Road)

*   **The Lyric:** *"Laying in the road / Was a shoe-in for the crash of the day / And we're never going to walk away"*
*   **The Connection:** He admits that his lifestyle made a catastrophic crash inevitable. 
*   **The Evolution:** The car crash evolved from a weaponized insult (**Seventy Times 7**) to a literal tragedy (**Limousine**) to a suicide attempt (**Handcuffs**). Here, he looks back at the wreckage and accepts his culpability. He was waiting for the crash.

#### 6. The Resolution of the Grave Motif (Climbing Out)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I'm hoping that in time, you can lay down / All this weight you've been carrying around... To climb on up out of your grave / With the bits of you you managed to save"*
*   **The Connection:** He tells himself to drop the stone and climb out of the hole he dug.
*   **The Evolution:** He finally takes off the weight from **Millstone** (*"I'm my own stone around my neck"*). He climbs out of the grave he spent all of *Daisy* digging (**At the Bottom**: *"I should've started some years ago digging that hole"*). The "bits of you" references the scattered pieces of himself from **Jaws Theme Swimming** (*"Cut up and left in bits and pieces"*). 

#### 7. The Resolution of the "Alone" Motif (The Mantra)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You are not alone, you are not alone / You are not alone, you get to reset"*
*   **The Connection:** The ultimate comfort. He repeats it like a mantra to drown out the fear.
*   **The Evolution:** This is the most profound thematic arc in the band's history. 
    *   He predicted he would die alone in **I Will Play My Game...**
    *   He panicked about dying alone in **Jesus Christ**. 
    *   He was alone in his bed in **At the Bottom**. 
    *   Here, he reclaims the ghostly outro from **Play Crack the Sky** (*"You know that you are not alone"*) and uses it to finally defeat his greatest fear. You are not alone, and you can reset the machine.

# Track 4: "Could Never Be Heaven" (*Science Fiction*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"Could Never Be Heaven" is the acoustic, emotional counterpart to "Play Crack the Sky" and "Jesus Christ." If those earlier songs were about the terror of dying alone and sinking in the water, this song is about finding peace at the bottom of the lake. It is a profound declaration of love and devotion to his family (his wife and children), confirming that real human connection is the only heaven he needs. The song officially ends the "Isolation" motif that plagued Jesse Lacey for 16 years.

### Lyrical Linkages & Database Connections:

#### 1. The Lake / Drowning in Peace

*   **The Lyric:** *"I was drowning in the lake, damned / Over a dead mining town / The cars and the homes lay still in the water / The deeper I sank, the less I died"*
*   **The Connection:** He returns to the bottom of the lake, but this time, sinking brings relief rather than death. He is submerged among the wreckage of normal life (cars and homes).
*   **The Evolution:** This is the same lake from **At the Bottom** (*"And there's a lake / And at the bottom you'll find all my friends"*). The execution by "cement galoshes" from **Luca** is reversed here. Instead of struggling for air, the deeper he sinks, the less he feels like he is dying. The water has finally become a place of comfort rather than doom. 

#### 2. Singing at the Bottom

*   **The Lyric:** *"There were people I love below me singing / And all of the songs were about you"*
*   **The Connection:** At the bottom of the lake, he finds loved ones singing about the person who saves him (his wife).
*   **The Evolution:** This directly answers the tragedy of **At the Bottom** (*Daisy*), where his friends were at the bottom of the lake, but *"they don't swim 'cause they're all dead."* Here, the people below him aren't dead; they are singing.

#### 3. The Death of God / The Whale

*   **The Lyric:** *"The whale is well-rehearsed / Swimming circles in the church / A Cardinal fish says, 'God is dead' / The whale says, 'Get out of my head'"*
*   **The Connection:** A surreal, aquatic theological debate. The church is submerged, the Cardinal (a church official / a fish) declares God is dead, and the whale demands peace and quiet.
*   **The Evolution:** This continues the "Empty Church" motif from **I Will Play My Game...** (*Deja*) and the "Silence of God" motif from **Millstone**. The declaration that "God is dead" finalizes the theological arc of the band. Jesse no longer expects salvation from the Bureaucracy of Heaven; he has found his own salvation in his family. 

#### 4. The End of Isolation (The Merging of Bodies)

*   **The Lyric:** *"You are not alone / We are not separate / My daughter's shoulders are my shoulders / My son's hands my hands / My wife's heart my own heart"*
*   **The Connection:** He explicitly repeats the mantra from the previous track ("You are not alone") and proves it by merging his own identity and anatomy with his family. 
*   **The Evolution:** This is the absolute defeat of the "Die Alone" prophecy from **Jesus Christ**. Furthermore, it completely resolves the "Blame/Children" motif. In **The Quiet Things** (*Deja*), he noted that *"parents let them shoulder all the blame."* In **Archers** (*TDAG*), he warned to *"order your daughters to ignore me."* Here, he vows to carry the burden for them—his daughter's shoulders are his own. 

#### 5. The Outer Darkness (Rejecting Heaven)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I've heard the outer darkness is really nice this time of year / Could never be heaven without you"*
*   **The Connection:** "Outer darkness" is a biblical term for Hell (or the void outside of Heaven). He declares that he would rather be in Hell with his family than in Heaven without them.
*   **The Evolution:** This directly answers the fear in **Jesus Christ** (*"I'm terrified I might go to hell"*). He is no longer terrified. He accepts the "outer darkness" because the presence of his loved ones is the only heaven he requires. 

#### 6. The Wizard of Oz (The Missing Pieces)

*   **The Lyric:** *"I have no heart, I have no brain / Lord, I have no courage / Can you get me home again?"*
*   **The Connection:** He references the Tin Man, the Scarecrow, the Lion, and Dorothy, identifying himself as someone missing all the crucial pieces of a human being, desperate to go "home."
*   **The Evolution:** This reinforces the "Anatomy/Missing Pieces" motif. He spent years taking his head apart (**Degausser**) and hardening his heart (**The Boy Who Blocked His Own Shot**). Now he is asking to be put back together so he can finally return to the domestic space (the "home" he locked himself out of in **Failure By Design**).

# Track 6: "137" (*Science Fiction*)

**Thematic Summary:** 
"137" takes the Brand New "Explosion/Fire" motif to its absolute, apocalyptic extreme. The title refers to Cesium-137, a radioactive isotope produced by nuclear fission. The song is a dark, brooding, and somewhat cynical lullaby about the inevitability of global nuclear war. Rather than fearing the apocalypse, Jesse Lacey embraces it, viewing nuclear annihilation as the ultimate, egalitarian solution to human suffering and the final answer to the "Bureaucracy of Heaven."

*   **The Evolution:** This is the macro-level conclusion to the "Fire/Explosion" motif.
